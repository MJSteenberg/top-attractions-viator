Green Grotto Caves Tours and Tickets

The Green Grotto Caves are a labyrinth of limestone caves located in Falmouth, between the resort towns of Ocho Rios and Montego Bay. Once used as shelter by the native Arawak Indians, the caves have since been employed as a hiding place for smugglers and runaway enslaved people, before serving as the site of a nightclub. Now, they're a Jamaican attraction you can tour in the company of a local guide.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Green Grotto Caves in 2024?
A:

Ticket prices for the Green Grotto Caves in Jamaica are US$20 for adults (ages 13 and over), with discounts for younger visitors and Jamaican residents. Guided tours of Jamaica that also include a stop at the Green Grotto Caves start at around US$150 and usually include admission fees.

Q:Is the Green Grotto Caves tour suitable for children?
A:

Yes, the Green Grotto Caves are a popular family-friendly attraction in Jamaica. Generally, children ages 4 and above can participate, although the tour is most suitable for kids who can easily walk on uneven surfaces. The tour offers educational insights into the natural and cultural history of Jamaica.

Q:How do I get to the Green Grotto Caves from Montego Bay?
A:

To get to the Green Grotto Caves from Montego Bay, you can take the A1 highway about 1.52 hours east toward Runaway Bay and Discovery Bay. To avoid the hassle of driving, book a private transfer or guided tour that includes roundtrip transportation and the entrance fee.

Q:What should I bring with me when visiting the Green Grotto Caves?
A:

When visiting the Green Grotto Caves, wear sturdy shoes with good grip, as the cave paths can be uneven and slippery. The caves are usually cooler in temperature than the outside air, so bring a light jacket. Also, dont forget insect repellent, since the caves are home to bugs.

Q:Are the Green Grotto Caves accessible to people with disabilities?
A:

No, the Green Grotto Caves are not fully accessible to people with disabilities. The caves have uneven and sometimes steep paths, and there are steps and areas with low ceilings that might make navigation difficult. However, paved paths lead to the entrance and some viewing areas that might be accessible.
