Chavón River (Río Chavón) Tours and Tickets

One of the largest rivers in the Dominican Republic, the Chavón River (Rio Chavón) runs from the interior of the country to the Caribbean Sea near La Romana. The filming site of several famous movies—including Apocalypse Now, Rambo, and Jurassic Park—it’s a popular stop on tours or river cruises.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Chavón River in 2024?
A:

There’s no cost to visit the Chavón River, but there are fees for activities such as river boat cruises, kayaking, canoeing, or fishing excursions. Half-day ATV and buggy tours through the jungle near the river start from around US$65, while full-day river cruises with a stop at Catalina Island begin from about $110.

Q:What is the Chavón River known for?
A:

The Chavón River is known for being one of the largest rivers in the Dominican Republic. It starts in the Cordillera Oriental mountains inland and leads into the Caribbean Sea near La Romana. Several movies have been filmed along the river, which is also a popular destination for river cruises.

Q:What activities can I do on the Chavón River?
A:

One of the most popular ways to experience the Chavón River is to take a river cruise, while other activities include kayaking, canoeing, and fishing. By land you can hike, go horseback riding, or ride ATVs or buggies through the lush jungle near the river.

Q:What is the best time to visit Río Chavón?
A:

For the best weather, time your visit to the Chavón River between December and April. This is also the high tourist season for the Dominican Republic, so book activities in advance and try to visit during the week if possible. Hurricane season generally occurs between June and October and can disrupt travel plans.

Q:What other attractions are near Río Chavón?
A:

Popular attractions near the Chavón River include Altos de Chavón, a replica of a 16th century Mediterranean village perched on the cliffs above the river, which offers one of the best views of the river. There’s also Catalina Island, just off the coast, near where the river meets the Caribbean Sea.
