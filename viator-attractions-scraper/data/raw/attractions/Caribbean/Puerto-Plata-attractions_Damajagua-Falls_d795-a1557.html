Hidden in a lush Dominican Republic jungle, Damajagua Falls—otherwise known as the 27 Waterfalls of Rio Damajagua or 27 Charcos—are a series of 27 cascading waterfalls that were discovered in the 1990s. Located in the midst of sugarcane fields in the Northern Corridor mountain range, the hidden falls are a true off-the-beaten-path experience.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Damajagua Falls in 2025?
A:

Admission prices vary depending on whether you visit seven, twelve, or 27 waterfalls and what your tour package includes. For example, a short hike and visit to the lower falls, plus Dominican food, might start at US$58 per person, while a package including ziplining and lunch costs US$120.

Q:What is Damajagua Falls known for?
A:

Located in a lush tropical forest in the northern Dominican Republic, Damajagua Falls is known for its series of 27 waterfalls. It’s a popular destination for adventure-seekers, as visitors can hike, swim in turquoise pools, slide down natural rock slides, and zipline through the treetops.

Q:What is the best month to visit Damajagua Falls?
A:

The waterfalls can be visited year-round, although they close during heavy rainfall. If you can, aim for the dry season from December to April. That’s when you find crystal-clear water and gentler currents. Arrive early if you can (by 10am), as the site fills up by midday.

Q:Can you go to Damajagua Falls on your own?
A:

Yes, you can reach the entrance independently by renting a car or taking a taxi, but you must use a guide to reach the falls. Entering alone is not permitted. A guide is necessary whether you get there with a convenient, organized excursion or in a private vehicle.

Q:Is Damajagua Falls good for kids?
A:

Yes. Older kids and teens will get a kick out of the falls, as it offers a high-energy mix of climbing, walking, and swimming. But give this one a miss if traveling with babies, toddlers, or younger tots, as Damajagua Falls is recommended for children 8 and up.
