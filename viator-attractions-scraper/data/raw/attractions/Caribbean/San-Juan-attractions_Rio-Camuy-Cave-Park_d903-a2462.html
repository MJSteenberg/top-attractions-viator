Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy) Tours and Tickets

Known for its soaring interiors, million-year-old stalactites and stalagmites, and namesake underground river, Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy) in Puerto Rico attracts nature lovers and casual visitors alike. The 268-acre (108-hectare) complex is most famous for its cave tour, but it also offers picnic pavilions, walking trails, and a playground.

Q:What is Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy) is a network of limestone caves in Puerto Rico carved by a subterranean river. The network stretches over 10 miles (16 kilometers), with more than 220 caves and caverns and features stalactites, stalagmites, sinkholes, and unique rock formations.
Q:How do I get to Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
Because public transportation options to the Rio Camuy Cave Park are limited, consider renting a car, taking a taxi, or booking a tour with roundtrip transportation from San Juan. You also can book a private car and driver. The drive from San Juan typically takes about 1.5–2 hours.
Q:What are the main attractions within Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
The main attractions within Rio Camuy Cave Park include Cueva Clara, the most prominent and accessible cave. It features enormous chambers adorned with stalactites, stalagmites, and other rock formations. There’s also a sinkhole called el Sumidero de Empalme where you can spot the underground river and a natural limestone bridge.
Q:Is there a guided tour available at Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
Yes, guided tours at Rio Camuy Cave Park offer educational insights into the cave system’s geology and history. They include a walking tour of Cueva Clara, the main cave accessible to the public. For the tour you’ll receive a safety helmet with a headlamp, an audio tour device, and headphones.
Q:Are there any age or physical requirements for visiting the caves (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
No, the tours are designed for visitors of all ages and fitness levels. The tour path follows concrete walkways with side rails; however, some parts may involve walking on uneven surfaces and climbing stairs. Rides to the entrance are available for people with disabilities, seniors, and travelers with small children.
Q:Are there any nearby attractions or activities to do after visiting Rio Camuy Cave Park (Parque Nacional de las Cavernas del Río Camuy)?
A:
Yes, nearby attractions include Cueva Ventana, a cave that offers a stunning view of the Arecibo Valley. And, in Utuado you’ll find Dos Bocas Lake, a reservoir surrounded by lush greenery. Boat operators offer rides around the lake, as well as rides to restaurants located on the shores.
