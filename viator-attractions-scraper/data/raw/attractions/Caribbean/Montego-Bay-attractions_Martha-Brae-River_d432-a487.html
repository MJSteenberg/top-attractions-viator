Q:How much does it cost to visit the Martha Brae River in 2024?
A:

Rafting trips on the Martha Brae River that include round-trip transportation and raft fees usually cost between US$140 and US$180, depending on where you're departing from and whether you go for private or group transportation. Tours typically include foot rubs using limestone from the river banks, and some include beer or coconut water served onboard.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit the Martha Brae River?
A:

The best time of year to visit the Martha Brae River is between December and April, during Jamaica’s dry season. May to November is the rainy season, with storms typically peaking in August, September, and October.

Q:How can I get to the Martha Brae River?
A:

The easiest way to get to the Martha Brae River is by car. If you book a tour from either Montego Bay or Ocho Rios, most will include private transportation and the drive is around one hour from each. The town of Falmouth takes approximately 10 minutes to reach.

Q:Is there an entry fee for Martha Brae River?
A:

While parts of the river are accessible for free, most people choose to visit the headwaters for raft trips. If booking a raft trip, remember to bring money for extra snacks or souvenirs.

Q:What activities can I do on the Martha Brae River?
A:

The main activity for visitors on the Martha Brae River is hopping aboard a traditional bamboo raft and letting your captain guide you down the river, from where you can spot wildlife and view the remarkable scenery. Before or after your journey, stop by Miss Martha's Herb Garden to learn about medicinal plants.

Q:What should I wear for a rafting trip on the Martha Brae River?
A:

Wear comfortable clothing that is suitable for warm weather and a hat for sun protection. Avoid heeled shoes, as you’ll need to be able to climb on and off the raft with ease. Remember your clothes and shoes might get wet, so plan what to wear accordingly.

Q:Is visiting the Martha Brae River worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting the Martha Brae River is worth it, especially if you enjoy spending time outside in nature. It’s generally peaceful and provides an alternative to the beach scenes in Montego Bay and Ocho Rios.
