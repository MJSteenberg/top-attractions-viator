Dunn's River Falls Tours and Tickets

Dunn's River Falls is a spectacular White River waterfall near Jamaica’s Ocho Rios. Here, refreshing, cold mountain water cascades 1,000 feet (300 meters) down naturally terraced steps. The way the world-famous falls renew themselves via regular deposits of calcium carbonate and sodium is fascinating.

You don’t even have to be a fan of geology to appreciate it. Meanwhile, movie buffs will recognize these iconic waterfalls from films that are even more iconic. For example, Dr. No, starring Sean Connery, and Cocktail, starring Tom Cruise.

Q:How long is a Dunn's River Falls tour?
A:

The length of Dunn's River Falls tours varies depending on where you're coming from. Many tours include stops at Ocho Rios Blue Hole or other attractions within the vicinity. If you're just visiting the falls, factor in about three hours for tours from Ocho Rios and five hours for tours from Montego Bay. This includes your travel time.

Q:What is the best day to visit Dunn's River Falls?
A:

The best day to visit Dunn's River Falls depends on the cruise ship schedule, though weekdays are generally less crowded than weekends. Days that get the most cruise ships in northern ports tend to get the crowds, as many people visit Dunn's River Falls on guided shore excursions. Note: if you’re visiting on a rainy day, expect the water to be slightly cooler. Still, it won’t be cold enough that you need a wetsuit. The water temperature is pretty consistent, and comfortable, regardless of the season.

Q:What should I bring to Dunn's River Falls?
A:

Bring your towel, swimsuit, sun protection, and any fresh clothing you want to change into after your swim. If you have water shoes and want to climb the falls, bring those. You won’t need them for snakes, because there aren’t any. However, you may want them for rocks. You can also rent or purchase water shoes upon arrival. If you want to store your belongings while you climb, you can rent a locker at the falls.

Q:How long does it take to climb Dunns River Falls?
A:

It usually takes people only an hour to climb Dunn's River Falls, though visitors in really good shape can make it up in under 45 minutes. That said, it's wise to give yourself at least a few hours at the falls to account for potential crowds and lines. You won’t want to be rushed while experiencing this natural phenomenon that took years to create.

Q:Can you see Dunn's River Falls without climbing?
A:

Yes, you can see Dunn's River Falls without climbing up them. While holding hands with other travelers and your guide and making your way up the cascades is a popular way to experience the falls, you can just as easily enjoy the waterfall's lower pool or take in its beauty from dry land. Many visitors just choose to relax without getting their heart rates up.

Q:Can you bring food to Dunn's River Falls?
A:

Yes, you can bring your own food to Dunn's River Falls, and many people do, as it's a great place to have a picnic. If you'd rather pack lightly, you can buy snacks, soft drinks, and even jerk chicken. Bring cash for these purchases in case the on-site vendors don’t accept credit cards.
