YS Falls Tours and Tickets

YS Falls comprises seven waterfalls on the YS River, located in St. Elizabeth Parish on the lush south coast of Jamaica. Often overshadowed by Dunn’s River Falls, YS is worth a visit for its more secluded location and the promise of a less-crowded experience of Jamaica’s natural beauty.

People Also Ask
Q:Is visiting YS Falls worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting YS Falls is worth it. It’s a fun alternative to swimming in the sea if you’re looking for a place to cool off, with natural pools fed by beautiful cascades. There are also plenty of great activities to take advantage of, from zipline adventures to jitney tractor rides.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit YS Falls?
A:

The best time of day to visit YS Falls is first thing in the morning when there are fewer crowds. Just be aware that the falls are closed on Mondays and public holidays, and entry closes when the falls reach capacity.

Q:What is the best month to visit YS Falls?
A:

The best time of year to visit YS Falls is between December and April when there’s less chance of rain. The rainy season brings stronger currents and slippery rocks, which can make visiting the falls less enjoyable. Note that the holiday season can get crowded.

Q:Is YS Falls accessible?
A:

No, YS Falls is not accessible, and you should be prepared to walk on uneven terrain. There are no facilities for visitors with mobility differences, and the area around the falls can be rocky and slippery. Take care when walking, and consider wearing water shoes over flip-flops or bare feet.

Q:Are facilities like restrooms or changing rooms available at YS Falls?
A:

Yes, there are facilities at YS Falls. The complex has restrooms and separate changing rooms, so you don't need to wear your swimsuit under your clothes—just pack a towel. Other facilities include a casual restaurant, Chill & Grill, and a gift shop selling souvenirs.

Q:Are there any nearby attractions or activities that complement a visit to YS Falls?
A:

Yes, there are plenty of attractions near YS Falls worth visiting, and many tours to YS Falls also stop at some other key attractions. These include the Appleton Estate, where you can taste Jamaican rum right at the source, and the Black River, popular for its boat safaris.
