Amid the lush islands and karst cliffs of the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Halong Bay; Surprise Cave (Hang Sung Sot) is one of the most memorable highlights. Q:What makes Surprise Cave (Hang Sung Sot) a must-visit attraction in Halong Bay?
A:

Discovered by French explorers in 1901, Surprise Cave is one of the largest and most spectacular caves in Halong Cave. Spanning around 108,000 square feet (10,000 square meters), it features massive caverns filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and karst formations in different shapes, lit by colored lights.

Q:How do I get to Surprise Cave from Hanoi?
A:

Surprise Cave is on Bo Hon Island in Halong Bay and is accessible by boat. Many Halong Bay boat tours, including day and multi-day cruises, include a stop at the cave and transfers from Hanoi. Alternatively, rent a boat to visit directly from Tuan Chau and Hong Gai Pier.

Q:Is there an entrance fee to visit Surprise Cave?
A:

Yes, Surprise Cave has an entrance fee, though most Halong Bay day cruises and overnight cruises that stop at the cave will already include the entrance fees as part of the tour. If you visit on your own, you will have to purchase a ticket to the cave.

Q:How long does it take to explore Surprise Cave?
A:

If you come on a Halong Bay boat cruise, you will likely have 60 to 90 minutes at Surprise Cave. If you’re visiting independently, you can spend as long as you want, though you may want to allot more time if you’re visiting over a busy period, like the afternoon.

Q:Is Surprise Cave accessible to visitors with disabilities?
A:

No, Surprise Cave is not accessible to visitors with mobility issues or in wheelchairs. You must climb around 50 steps from the dock area to the entrance to access it. Then, you must walk through a narrow passageway connecting different chambers, which may not be suitable if you experience claustrophobia.

Q:When is the best time to visit Surprise Cave?
A:

One of the busiest times at Surprise Cave is the early afternoon when many day cruises stop at the cave. Opt for an overnight cruise that stops at the cave in the morning or visit independently early in the morning. Visit in winter for less humidity.
