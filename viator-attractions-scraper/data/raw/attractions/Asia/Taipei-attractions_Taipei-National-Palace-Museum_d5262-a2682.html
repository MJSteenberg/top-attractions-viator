The National Palace Museum in Taipei is home to one of the most important collections of Chinese art in the world, and covers more than 8,000 years of Chinese history and culture. Featuring some 690,000 pieces, it covers all areas of Chinese art, including antiquities, painting, calligraphy, bronzes, jade, ceramics, and sculpture.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Taipei National Palace Museum in 2024?
A:

General admission tickets to the Taipei National Palace Museum are currently NT$350 for adults, NT$150 for students and residents, and NT$75 for seniors. Children under 18, students who are citizens, and visitors with disabilities receive free admission. There are five free admission days annually and the Zhishan Garden is free to visit.

Q:What is the history of the artifacts in the Taipei National Palace Museum?
A:

Most of the collection showcased in the Taipei National Palace Museum once formed the imperial art collection in Beijings Forbidden Palace. When the Japanese invaded northern China in the early 1930s, the collections were removed and hidden in southern China. In 1949, the Nationalists moved 4,800 cases to Taiwan, where they were hidden until the National Palace Museum opened in 1965.

Q:Are there guided tours available at the Taipei National Palace Museum?
A:

Yes, there are a variety of tour options at the Taipei National Palace Museum. Visitors can rent an audio guidetheres a version for both adults and children in a variety of languages. Guided tours are also available: twice a day in Mandarin, and twice a day in English. There are several themed tour routes for self-guided tours.

Q:How do you get to the Taipei National Palace Museum using public transportation?
A:

The Taipei National Palace Museum is located in Taipeis Shilin District. You can take the MRT Red Line to Shilin station, then buses R30, 255, M1, 855, S18, 304, S19, or 300; MRT Brown Line to Dazhi station, then bus BR13; or MRT Brown Line to Jiannan Rd station, followed by buses BR20 or BL7. The Taipei Sightseeing Bus also stops at the museum.

Q:Is the Taipei National Palace Museum wheelchair accessible?
A:

The Taipei National Palace Museum is suitable for wheelchair users; an accessible route through the museum is marked, wheelchairs can be borrowed from the checkroom, and there are accessible elevators and restrooms. Visitors with disabilities and one caregiver receive free admission.
