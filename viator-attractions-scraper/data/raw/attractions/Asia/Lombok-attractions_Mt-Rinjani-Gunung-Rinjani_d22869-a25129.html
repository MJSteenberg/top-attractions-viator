Mt. Rinjani (Gunung Rinjani) Tours and Tickets

When volcanic and seismic activity permits, 12,224-foot (3,726-meter) Mt. Rinjani is one of Indonesia’s great volcano climbs—even if you stop, as many climbers do, at the crater rim. The towering peak, complete with crater lake, dominates north Lombok, so even when the mountain is closed to visitors, hikes on the lower slopes appeal.

People Also Ask
Q:What is the best time to climb Mount Rinjani for ideal weather and clear views?
A:
The best time to visit Mt. Rinjani is on full moon nights, considered particularly auspicious for Lombok's Sasak people and Balinese Hindus. Try to plan your visit for a clear day and note that the mountain is often closed to visitors during the rainy season, which runs from December through March.
Q:Are there guided tours available for trekking to the summit of Mount Rinjani?
A:
Yes, there are guided trekking tours to the summit of Mt. Rinjani, and climbing the mountain with a guide is the way to go. Different routes are available depending on how long you have and your fitness level, but most are either 1- or 2-night adventures.
Q:What is the recommended preparation and training for visitors planning to climb Mount Rinjani?
A:
Trekking to the top of Mt. Rinjani requires decent physical fitness, but it's best to consult your doctor to know what's right for you. Many people prepare by running regularly and hitting the gym, and practicing treks will help you gain endurance and strengthen your leg and back muscles.
Q:Can I combine a Mount Rinjani trek with visits to nearby attractions or islands in Lombok?
A:
Yes, you can combine a Mount Rinjani trek with visits to nearby attractions. While many visitors to Lombok come specifically for the chance to ascend Rinjani, there's plenty more to do on the island, from hiking to the Sindang Gila and Tiu Kelep Waterfalls to exploring rice terraces.
Q:What amenities are typically included in Mount Rinjani tour packages?
A:
Mt. Rinjani trekking tour packages typically include the services of an English-speaking guide, professional porters, meals, and camping gear. You will generally be expected to bring your own daypack, shoes, and a headlight, but porters will often carry the bulk of your gear along with tents, sleeping bags, food, cooking equipment, and even drinking water.
Q:What are the different trekking routes and durations available for Mount Rinjani tours?
A:
Most tours to the top of Mount Rinjani last either two days and one night or three days and two nights. There are two routes up to the summit: the Sembalun and Senaru Route. Some people use one to ascend and the other to descend.
