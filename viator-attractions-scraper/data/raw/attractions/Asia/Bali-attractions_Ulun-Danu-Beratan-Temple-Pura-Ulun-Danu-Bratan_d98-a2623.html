Ulun Danu Beratan Temple (Pura Ulun Danu Bratan) Tours and Tickets

Set on the shores of Lake Bratan (Danau Bratan), close to the town of Bedugul, Pura Ulun Danu Bratan is one of Bali’s most photographed temples. Built, like Taman Ayun Temple, by the king of Mengwi, the combination of multi-roofed shrines with mountains and reflecting lake is incredibly photogenic—one reason it’s so popular with tourists.

Q:What is Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, and where is it located in Bali?
A:

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Dewi Danu, the Goddess of the Water. It’s on the western shores of Lake Beratan in the Bedugul highlands of north-central Bali. It's around 1.5 hours by car from Ubud and 2-3 hours from Kuta, Nusa Dua, and Seminyak.

Q:How do I get to Ulun Danu Beratan Temple from popular tourist areas like Ubud or Seminyak?
A:

Ulun Danu Bratan temple is located in Bedugul. You can drive or arrange a car and driver or taxi. Many sightseeing tours of central and north Bali include a stop at the temple. You can also take the Perama shuttle bus from Kuta, Ubud, Sanur, Lovina, Padang Bai, or Candidasa.

Q:Is there an entrance fee to visit Ulun Danu Beratan Temple?
A:

Yes, there is an entrance fee for both adults and children to visit Ulun Danu Beratan Temple. There is also a parking fee for visitors who drive, with different prices for cars and motorbikes. Within the temple complex, there is also a fee for using the restroom.

Q:What is the best time to visit Ulun Danu Beratan Temple?
A:

The wet season (November to April) is the best time to visit Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, as the lake's water levels will be higher, and the temple is more likely to appear floating on the water. Visit early in the morning to beat the crowds.

Q:Is there a specific dress code or other requirements for visiting Ulun Danu Beratan Temple?
A:

Yes. Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is an important and active place of worship. Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully and cover their shoulders and knees. Sarongs and scarves are helpful and may be available at the entrance. Women should not visit while menstruating.

Q:Are photography or drone flights allowed at Ulun Danu Beratan Temple?
A:

Yes and no. While photography is allowed at Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, drones are not. Sunrise and sunset offer the best lighting. Take a boat ride on the lake for different photographic perspectives on the temple, and bring a tripod, neutral density filters, and circular polarizer filter for better photos.
