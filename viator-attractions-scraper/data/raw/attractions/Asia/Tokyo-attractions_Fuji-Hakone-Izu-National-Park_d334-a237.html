People Also Ask
Q:What are the main attractions in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park?
A:

Mt. Fuji (Fuji-san) is the best-known attraction in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park, but it's not the only draw for many visitors. Other popular attractions include the Hakone Shrine, the Hakone Open-Air Museum, and the Komagatake Ropeway. Dont miss Mt. Fuji's 5th Station, which offers great views of the surrounding area.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park?
A:

The park is lovely year-round, while each season has its own special draws. If you're coming for Mt. Fuji views, consider a winter visit, when the skies are typically clearer. Summer offers great weather for outdoor activities, while spring brings beautiful blooms. Come in the fall for a cooler climate and fall foliage.

Q:How can I get to Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park from Tokyo?
A:

The easiest way to get to Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park from Tokyo is via public transit or tour. There are a number of options by train, including the Odakyu Electric Railway from Shinjuku Station to Hakone-Yumoto Station, or board a bullet train for around 30-minutes from Tokyo to Odawara, where you can transfer to the Hakone Tozan Railway. Alternatively, join a full-day tour from Tokyo that includes private car transfers from your hotel.

Q:Are there guided tours of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park?
A:

Yes, there are guided tours of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Parkgoing with a guide is a great idea if you havent been to the park before or dont speak Japanese. Private tours are popular and are typically customizable to suit your interests. Many tours include round-trip transportation from Tokyo, too.

Q:What activities can I do in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park?
A:

Hiking is one of the most popular activities in Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park, though you don't have to be super outdoorsy to enjoy what the park has to offer. You can take in views of Mt. Fuji, learn about the area at its visitor centers, and even soak in natural hot springs.

Q:What should I pack for a trip to Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park?
A:

If youre visiting Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park on a day trip from Tokyo, you wont need to bring too much with you. Make sure to wear comfortable shoes that are suitable for light hiking and layers in case of shifts in the weather.
