People Also Ask
Q:Is the Huanghuacheng Great Wall worth visiting?
A:
Yes, this remote and picturesque stretch of the Great Wall is the only section that borders on water; part of the wall is submerged beneath the nearby lake. In addition, the Huanghuacheng Great Wall is much quieter than wall sections closer to Beijing, so you can enjoy the site without the crowds.
Q:How much does it cost to visit the Huanghuacheng Great Wall?
A:
Tickets to the Huanghuacheng Great Wall are CN¥45, making it slightly more expensive to visit than other wall sections near Beijing. Unless you join a tour with transportation included, you will also need round-trip transport from Beijing by bus (around CN¥50) or by taxi (from CN¥700 to CN¥1,400).
Q:How long does it take to walk the Huanghuacheng Great Wall?
A:
The wall section between Huanghuacheng and Xishuiyu is about 5 miles (8 kilometers) and takes about 3.5 to 4 hours to finish. This section of the Great Wall has stretches in disrepair and steep descents and descents that can be a challenge to walk along and slow your pace.
Q:When is the best time of year to visit the Huanghuacheng Great Wall?
A:
The Huanghuacheng Great Wall is named for the yellow wildflowers that blanket the nearby slopes during the summer, making it particularly striking to visit from April to October. The weather is cooler in the spring and fall, ideal for hiking this site with little or no shade.
Q:What should I wear to visit the Huanghuacheng Great Wall?
A:
This tall section of the Great Wall is very exposed to the elements, so wear a hat, sunscreen, sunglasses, and dress for the weather on the day you visit. One of the most challenging stretches to hike, the Huanghuacheng Great Wall, requires sturdy footwear that can take on the uneven surface.
Q:Is Badaling and Mutianyu or the Huanghuacheng Great Wall better?
A:
The Badaling and Mutianyu stretches of the Great Wall are better for visitors with limited time who do not want a challenging hike since they are both easier to reach and better preserved than Huanghuacheng. The Huanghuacheng Great Wall is ideal for avid hikers who want to avoid big crowds.
