Ba Be National Park Tours and Tickets

Established in 1992 as Vietnam’s eighth national park, Ba Be National Park protects nearly 39 square miles (100 square kilometers of rainforest, lakes, limestone peaks, caves, waterfalls, and ethnic minority villages. The park also boasts diverse natural biodiversity—it's home to 550 plant species and 65 different types of mammals.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Ba Be National Park in 2024?
A:

Ba Be National Park entry currently costs 70,000 dong for foreign visitors, while boat rides start from around 500,000 dong per group. Guided tours of the park can be anywhere from US$165 to upwards of US$260 per person, typically for three or four days in the park. Many tours include accommodation and transportation.

Q:How do I get to Ba Be National Park from Hanoi?
A:

The easiest way to get to Ba Be National Park from Hanoi is by joining a tour that includes round-trip transportation. Expect the drive to take around three hours each way. Bus services from Hanoi are also available, but the journey can be around five hours.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit Ba Be National Park?
A:

The best time of year to visit Ba Be National Park is during the dryer seasons: either March to May, or September to November. Alternatively, come during the Long Tong Festival—taking place after the first lunar month of the year—to participate in local cultural celebrations.

Q:Can I stay overnight in Ba Be National Park?
A:

Yes, you can stay overnight in Ban Be National Park. A range of options are available, including resorts, hotels, homestays, and guest houses. Accommodation is included in many park tours, or you can book independently through online travel agencies.

Q:What are the best activities to do in Ba Be National Park?
A:

There are lots of great things to do in Ba Be National Park, from hiking through rainforests to visiting Ban Gioc Waterfall. Many tours also take travelers to local villages to learn about the cultures of the communities who live in the area. Boating is also very popular, as is birding and wildlife-watching.
