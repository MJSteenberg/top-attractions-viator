Kashi Vishwanath Temple (Golden Temple) Tours and Tickets

Varanasi’s Kashi Vishwanath Temple is also called the Golden Temple, thanks to its pure gold spire and dome. The current building dates from 1780, but temples stood in the same place for centuries. Dedicated to the Hindu Lord Shiva—one of the most significant deities in the Hindu pantheon—Kashi Vishwanath is a major pilgrimage site.

Q:What is the historical significance of the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi?
A:

Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is among the most important Hindu temples in India. It is one of 12 temples in Varanasi where Shiva, the Hindu god of destruction, is believed to have appeared as a column of light. The current structure was built in the 18th century.

Q:What rituals and ceremonies take place at the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi?
A:

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi hosts ceremonies dedicated to Lord Shiva. Aarti ceremonies, where lamps are waved before the deity, are performed multiple times a day, accompanied by chanting and music. Special festivals like Mahashivratri are celebrated with elaborate rituals, processions, and prayers.

Q:Is there a dress code for visiting the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi?
A:

When entering a Hindu temple, it’s customary to dress modestly: cover your shoulders and knees, remove your shoes and socks, and leave them on the provided shoe racks. Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi doesn't have an official dress code, though pilgrims and devotees may have to follow separate regulations.

Q:Can I take photos or videos inside the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi?
A:

No, you can't take photos or videos inside the Kashi Vishwanath Temple. Cameras and phones are not allowed inside the temple, though there are lockers outside the temple gates where you can store belongings for a fee. You may, however, take photographs of the temple exterior and the surrounding area.

Q:Is the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi accessible?
A:

No, the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is not accessible. The narrow lanes leading to the temple are often crowded and pose challenges for travelers in wheelchairs or with mobility issues. Ongoing efforts aim to enhance accessibility, but you should check directly with the temple before visiting.

Q:When is the best time of day to visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple?
A:

The best time to visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is during the performance of aartis and other rituals. The temple is usually open from 2:30am to 11pm, with aarti ceremonies held five times daily from 3am to 30 minutes before closing—check with the temple for the schedule before visiting.
