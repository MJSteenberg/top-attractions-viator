Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang) Tours and Tickets

Chairman Mao Memorial Hall, also known as the Mausoleum of Mao Zedong, houses the embalmed body of the revolutionary and dictator who was the founding father of the People’s Republic of China. The Soviet-style structure and the sculptures in front of it are one of the dominant features of Beijing’s Tiananmen Square.

People Also Ask
Q:What is the Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang) known for?
A:
Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang) is where the former Chinese leader Chairman Mao’s body lies. It’s the focal point of Tiananmen Square, so visitors to the area can’t miss it. It was built in 1977 after Mao’s death and is open to the public for viewing.
Q:When can I visit Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang)?
A:
You can visit Chairman Mao Memorial Hall in the mornings and afternoons, usually from 8am to 12pm and 2pm to 5pm. The hall typically is closed Mondays and Fridays and on some national holidays. Arrive early in the day to procure one of the limited, free tickets allowed each day.
Q:Is there a fee to enter Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang)?
A:
No, there isn’t a fee to enter Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang), but visitors still must get a ticket before they enter. There are a set amount of tickets issued each day, so there’s a chance you might not get to visit on the day you plan.
Q:What kinds of tours visit Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang)?
A:
Both private and group tours include the Chairman Mao Memorial Hall in Beijing day tours, which often go to the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, Temple of Heaven, and the Summer Palace. Some tours add the Great Wall or a rickshaw tour of Beijing’s famous alleys (hutongs).
Q:How do I get to Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang)?
A:
It’s not difficult to get to Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang). It’s located in the heart of the famous Tiananmen Square, easily accessible by subway. Line 1 takes you to Tiananmen East or Tiananmen West stations. Or, you can head to Qianmen station on Line 2.
Q:Is Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang) worth it?
A:
It depends. If you are a history buff or keen on seeing embalmed bodies, then you may find a visit to Chairman Mao Memorial Hall (Maozhuxi Jinian Tang) is worth it. Often Beijing’s top attractions are crowded, and visiting the memorial hall can be hectic.
