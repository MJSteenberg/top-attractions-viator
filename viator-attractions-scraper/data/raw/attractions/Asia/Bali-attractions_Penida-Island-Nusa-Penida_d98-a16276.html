Nusa Penida (Penida Island) Tours and Tickets

4,379 reviews

Considered to be the Bali that time forgot for its unspoiled landscape, Nusa Penida (Penida Island) sits about 10 miles (15 kilometers) off the Bali coast, alongside the islands of Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan. Besides snorkeling and diving—the island is known for mantas and mola-molas (the world’s largest bony fish)—Penida offers unspoilt villages, rugged landscapes, and sacred temples.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Penida Island (Nusa Penida) in 2024?
A:

There’s an entrance fee to visit Penida Island, which is to help support local conservation efforts. It costs around US$1.50 for adults (or 25K rupiah) and US$1 for children (15K rupiah). From Bali, full-day sightseeing tours of the island start from US$50.

Q:What are the must-see attractions on Penida Island (Nusa Penida)?
A:

Penida Island’s most recognizable landmark is Kelingking Beach, thanks to the T-Rex shape of the headland framing the white-sand beach. Other must-see attractions include Diamond Beach and Crystal Bay, both fringed by palm trees, and the natural infinity pools of Angel’s Billabong.

Q:Can you visit Penida Island on a day trip from Bali, or should I plan to stay overnight?
A:

While it’s easy to visit Penida Island (Nusa Penida) on a day trip from Bali, staying overnight lets you enjoy the island after the daytime crowds have left. You can also get up early to visit popular spots and avoid long lines for photos. Accommodation options tend to be more expensive than on the main island.

Q:What should I bring with me when visiting Penida Island?
A:

When visiting Penida Island (Nusa Penida), you should bring some sturdy sneakers as the staircases down to some of the beaches are very steep. If you’re staying overnight, don’t forget mosquito repellant—the insects are most prevalent around sunset. Make sure you have plenty of cash as well, since there aren’t many ATMs on the island.

Q:Where do ferries going to Nusa Penida depart from on Bali?
A:

On Bali, the most frequent and fastest ferries depart from Sanur port in the south of the island; the ride lasts around 30 minutes depending on sea conditions and drops you off at the Banjar Nyuh port on Nusa Penida. You can also take the ferry from Kusumba port on Bali—this only takes 15 minutes.
