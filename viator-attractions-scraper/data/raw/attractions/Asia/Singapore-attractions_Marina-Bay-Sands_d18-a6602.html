People Also Ask
Q:Can you go to the Marina Bay Sands without being a guest?
A:
Yes, you can go to Marina Bay Sands without being a guest. The complex at the base of the building houses restaurants, stores, a casino, theater, and more, while the Observation Deck at SkyPark on the 56th floor offers views. However, the rooftop infinity pool is for guests only.
Q:What is the best time to visit Marina Bay Sands?
A:
Evening is a great time to visit Marina Bay Sands. The free sound, light, and water show, Spectra, plays at 8pm and 9pm nightly, with a further showing at 10pm on Fridays and Saturdays. The shops, restaurants, theater, and casino stay open late, while the after-dark skyline views are spectacular.
Q:How long do you need at Marina Bay Sands?
A:
You could spend anywhere from 20 minutes to a day at Marina Bay Sands. You might stay from morning till late enjoying retail therapy and fine dining, followed by gaming, a show, or the nightclub. Or you could whizz up the SkyPark Observation Deck for a few minutes of viewing.
Q:What is so special about Marina Bay Sands?
A:
Standing proud above the bay from which it takes its name, Marina Bay Sands is a Singapore icon. With three towers linked by the banana-shaped SkyPark, it’s the city-state’s single most distinctive building. The rooftop infinity pool offers sweeping skyline views that make it perhaps Singapore’s most in-demand Instagram location.
Q:Is there a dress code for Marina Bay Sands?
A:
It depends. Some parts of Marina Bay Sands have a dress code; others don’t. Bare feet are prohibited on the Observation Deck. Most entertainment venues, including Marquee nightclub, operate a smart-casual dress code, meaning men should wear long pants and closed-toe shoes and everyone should avoid pool or gym attire.
Q:Is Marina Bay Sands accessible?
A:
Yes, Marina Bay Sands is accessible—so accessible that it hosted the ASEAN Para Games athletes. The hotel offers a range of wheelchair-accessible rooms. The shops, theater, casino, Observation Deck, Cé La Vi Club Lounge, and SkyBar on the 56th floor are roller-friendly with accessible bathrooms.
