Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project Tours and Tickets

Situated in Kosgoda on Sri Lanka’s southwest coast, the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project works to protect endangered sea turtles by conserving nesting sites and providing a hatchery for rescued eggs. Visitors can tour the small, beachside facility to see the turtles, learn about their lifecycle, and discover the project’s work.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project in 2025?
A:

Admission tickets to the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project currently cost LKR 1,000 for foreign visitors and LKR 500 for Sri Lankan citizens. Alternatively, join a day tour that stops at the project; tours start from around US$60 and typically visit multiple sights, such as Galle Fort Lighthouse, or include activities like a Madu River Safari.

Q:How do I get to the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project?
A:

The Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project is about an hour by car from Galle and a 90-minute drive from Colombo. If you don’t have a vehicle, it’s easiest to join a tour that includes round-trip transportation. Buses run along the coast from Galle to Maha Indurawa, stopping a 15-minute walk from the project.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project?
A:

If your itinerary allows, the best time to visit the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project is in the middle of the night, when volunteers release turtles into the sea—season and weather permitting. Mornings are typically quietest for a visit, as you're less likely to encounter large groups of visitors.

Q:Can visitors participate in turtle release programs at the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project?
A:

Yes, visitors can participate in turtle release programs at the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project. If you're a casual visitor, then your participation will be limited to attending a turtle release. Volunteers have the chance to be involved more significantly, but typically stay between 1–12 weeks.

Q:Is visiting the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project is worth it. Not only does your visit help support the rehabilitation of turtles, it's also an excellent place to see baby sea turtles, learn about the threats to their native habitat, and better understand what efforts are needed to protect them.
