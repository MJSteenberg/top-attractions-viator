Sekumpul Waterfall (Air Terjun Sekumpul) Tours and Tickets

508 reviews

Spilling down from dizzying heights of up to 260 feet (80 meters), Sekumpul Waterfall, a series of seven narrow cascades, is hidden away amid lush tropical foliage. Dirt paths lead to elevated viewpoints overlooking the falls and to rock pools at the foot, where visitors can cool off with a refreshing swim.

  • Sawan, Sudaji, Bali

People Also Ask
Q:Is Sekumpul Waterfall worth visiting?
A:
Yes, Sekumpul Waterfall is worth visiting. It's a beautiful sight, with gorgeous cascades that appear to tumble straight out of rainforest foliage. The waterfall is particularly inviting on hot days when its a great place to cool off with a refreshing dip.
Q:Where is Sekumpul Waterfall located, and how do I get there?
A:
Sekumpul Waterfall is located in northern Bali, near the village of Buleleng. To get there, youll need first to reach Buleleng and then hike up to the waterfalls. The easiest way to get there is by joining a tour that includes transportation from your hotel.
Q:What are the best times of day to visit Sekumpul Waterfall?
A:
The best time of day to visit Sekumpul Waterfall is first thing in the morning, particularly if you want to avoid crowds. However, if you dont mind being around many people, you might find the weather is a bit warmer during the latter part of the day.
Q:How challenging is the trek to Sekumpul Waterfall?
A:
It depends. While the hike itself is not incredibly taxing, it comes with obstacles. If it's been raining, the path to the waterfall can get slippery and muddy, making it a bit more challenging. You'll also need to cross a stream at one point and hike over slippery rocks.
Q:Do you need a guide for Sekumpul Waterfall?
A:
No, you do not need a guide for Sekumpul Waterfall. However, going with a guide is a much safer bet, as they can help you navigate the trails and tell you what sort of fauna and foliage you see. As a bonus, guided tours typically include transportation, too.
