Lake Batur (Danau Batur) Tours and Tickets

Backed by the volcanic slabs of Mount Batur and Mount Abang, Lake Batur (Danau Batur) is a striking crater lake considered sacred by the Balinese. Besides sunrise trekking, Mount Batur offers hot springs, canoe trips, and tours of Trunyan village, where the local Bali Aga people expose their dead to rot in the open air.

Q:How much does visiting Lake Batur (Danau Batur) in 2024 cost?
A:

Although there is no fee to visit Lake Batur (Danau Batur), there is an entrance fee for the Kintamani area, which the lake is a part of. The fees are Rp50,000 for foreign tourists, Rp20,000 for domestic tourists, and Rp10,000 for local Bali visitors. You can rent a kayak on the lake for around US$10.

Q:What are the best activities to do at Lake Batur?
A:

Some of the most popular activities at Lake Batur include enjoying the views of the lake from a Mt. Batur sunrise trek or a restaurant above the lake, going on a boat tour, canoeing or kayaking, fishing, or soaking in one of the volcanic hot springs around the lake.

Q:When is the best time to visit Lake Batur?
A:

The best time of the year to visit Lake Batur is during the dry season, between May and September. The lake is most beautiful then, and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. For lake activities, its best to visit in the morning, when the water tends to be calm.

Q:Are there any local restaurants or food options around Lake Batur?
A:

Yes, there are many restaurant options around Lake Batur. Most options are available around the west and southwest shores, and several floating restaurants are available. Several restaurants are set high above the lake, some of which offer expansive views of the lake.

Q:Is it possible to hike around Lake Batur?
A:

Yes, it is possible to hike around Lake Batur. The 15.7-mile (25.2-kilometer) loop trail takes a full day to complete. The Mt. Batur sunrise hike is the most popular nearby option and offers views of Lake Batur. Other hikes include Trunyan Hill, Trunyan Hill, Abang Mountain, and Gunung Agan.
