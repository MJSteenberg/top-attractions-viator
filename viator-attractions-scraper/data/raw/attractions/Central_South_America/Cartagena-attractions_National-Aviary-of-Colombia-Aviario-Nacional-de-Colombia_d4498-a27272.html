National Aviary of Colombia (Aviario Nacional de Colombia) Tours and Tickets

396 reviews

Home to around 190 different species of bird, the National Aviary of Colombia harbors diverse flora and more than 2,000 birds. The 17-acre (7-hectare) park categorises birds according to three Colombian ecosystems—tropical rainforest, coastal zone, and desert—and promises an enriching experience for wildlife lovers.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the National Aviary of Colombia in 2025?
A:

It costs 80K pesos for adults to visit the National Aviary of Colombia in 2025, while it’s 58K pesos for children aged 2–12 years old and students with valid ID. There’s a significant discount for Colombian nationals, although proof of nationality is requested. You can book tickets in advance on the official website.

Q:What bird species can you expect to see at the National Aviary of Colombia?
A:

At the National Aviary of Colombia you can see bird species including macaws, parrots, toucans, woodpeckers, Andean condors, harpy eagles, cormorans, and fochas. The most colorful creatures include American flamingos, the scarlet ibis, and birds native to the tropical rainforest.

Q:Is the National Aviary family-friendly and are there activities for children?
A:

Yes, the National Aviary of Colombia is the perfect place for children, especially if they’re interested in nature. The sightseeing loop is easy to follow and educational kiosks are an engaging way for kids to learn more. The center also hosts talks and demonstrations.

Q:Are there any restaurants or cafés inside or near the National Aviary?
A:

Yes, there are several places to eat and drink in the National Aviary of Colombia. There are three coffee shops in the park, which serve hot and cold drinks and snacks, and a restaurant where you can enjoy meals of traditional Colombian and international cuisine.

Q:How long should you spend at the National Aviary of Colombia?
A:

It’s recommended to spend around 2.5 hours exploring the National Aviary of Colombia. Try to arrive in the morning or late afternoon to avoid the peak hours of heat, as the park can get extremely hot.
