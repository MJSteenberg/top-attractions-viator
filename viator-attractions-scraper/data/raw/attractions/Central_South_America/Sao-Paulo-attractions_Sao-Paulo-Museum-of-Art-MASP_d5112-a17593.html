Q:How much does it cost to visit the Sao Paulo Museum of Art (MASP) in 2025?
A:

Current ticket prices for the Sao Paulo Museum of Art (MASP) are R$75 per adult, while students with valid IDs and seniors pay R$37. Entry is free for disabled visitors and a companion, as well as children under 10, while entry is free for all on Tuesdays. Tickets can be purchased online.

Q:What are the opening hours of MASP?
A:

MASP is open every day except Mondays, from mid-morning until early evening. The museum offers free admission on Tuesdays, when opening hours are slightly extended. Note that the museum is closed annually on Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, New Year's Eve, and New Year's Day.

Q:How do I get to MASP using public transportation?
A:

To get to MASP using public transportation, you can take Line 2 (the Green Line) of the Metro to Trianon-MASP station, located just moments away on foot. Alternatively, dozens of local bus routes frequent the area; MASP is located on Avenida Paulista, one of the cityâs major thoroughfares.

Q:What are the best exhibitions or permanent collections to see at MASP?
A:

MASP was the first modern art museum in Brazil, and its 11,000-strong permanent collection contains one of the most important collections of European art in the southern hemisphere, including works by Raphael, Van Gogh, Picasso, and beyond. The museum also hosts an ever-changing array of temporary exhibitions that celebrate boundary-pushing modern and contemporary art.

Q:Is MASP accessible for people with disabilities?
A:

Yes, MASP is accessible for visitors with disabilities. The museum is barrier-free and elevators connect its five different levels, making it easy for wheelchair users to visit, as well as parents with strollers. Disabled visitors also receive free admission to the museum.
