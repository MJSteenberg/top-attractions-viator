Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse Tours and Tickets

Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse is often mistaken for “The Lighthouse at the End of the World,” made famous by novelist Jules Verne. Verne was actually inspired by the San Juan de Salvamento lighthouse, further east, but it’s a fitting description nonetheless: Les Eclaireurs is the last landmark many sailors see as they sail south to Antarctica.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse in 2025?
A:

While it's free to see Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse, you’ll need to take a boat to pass by this landmark and see it up close. Half-day cruises along the Beagle Channel start at around US$75; most boat tours visit multiple sites and offer the chance to spot wildlife, too.

Q:What is Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse, and why is it famous?
A:

Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse is a red and white lighthouse located in the Beagle Channel. The stone structure was built in 1919; since then it’s been known as the last man-made landmark that many sailors see before they head to Antarctica. The lighthouse remains in operation today, but is uninhabited and works via remote control.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse?
A:

The best time of year to visit Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse is typically during the southern hemisphere’s summer months of December to January, when you’ll enjoy the mildest weather. Summer is the peak season, so make your tour reservations in advance.

Q:Are there any wildlife viewing opportunities near Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse?
A:

Yes, there are wildlife viewing opportunities near Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse. The lighthouse is often surrounded by sea lions, as well as seabirds, such as cormorants, albatrosses, and petrels. Several boat tours include a visit to the penguin colony of Isla Martillo.

Q:What should I bring on a tour to Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse?
A:

A list of things to bring on a tour to Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse should include a waterproof jacket, gloves, and a hat—remember, conditions are colder out on the water. Pack layers that you can take on and off, as well as sun protection, including sunscreen and sunglasses.
