Old Shoes Monument (Los Zapatos Viejos) Tours and Tickets

475 reviews

Located at the base of San Felipe Castle, the Old Shoes Monument (Los Zapatos Viejos) is a giant sculpture of a pair of old boots. A popular spot for a selfie, the monument was created by Hector Lombana Piñeres in response to the poem “Mi Ciudad Nativa” by local poet (and one of South America’s most respected writers) Luis Carlos López.

  • Calle 31 No. 18B-55, Cartagena, Bolivar

Q:How much does it cost to visit Old Shoes Monument (Los Zapatos Viejos) in 2025?
A:

The Old Shoes Monument is free to visit. Cartagena city tours that pass by or stop at the monument start at US$40. Lively night tours by chiva (party bus) start at US$12 and allow you to admire the bronze shoes illuminated at night.

Q:Is the Old Shoes Monument (Los Zapatos Viejos) worth it?
A:

Yes, the Old Shoes Monument is worth visiting if you're on your way to see San Felipe de Barajas Castle (Castillo San Felipe de Barajas). It's an excellent photo opportunity, and the castle serves as a dramatic backdrop for the bronze sculpture designed by Colombian artist Hector Lombana.

Q:Are there any nearby attractions or landmarks to visit after seeing the Old Shoes Monument?
A:

Yes, there are several attractions near the Old Shoes Monument. It is a 3-minute walk from San Felipe de Barajas Castle (Castillo San Felipe de Barajas) and a 20-minute walk from Cartagena's colorful Old Town, all of which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Q:Is the Old Shoes Monument wheelchair-accessible?
A:

Yes, the Old Shoes Monument is wheelchair-accessible. The plaza surrounding it is mostly flat and even. A small curb surrounds the sculpture, but it should not pose much of a problem for wheelchairs and strollers.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit the Old Shoes Monument to avoid crowds?
A:

The best time to visit the Old Shoes Monument to avoid the crowds is early morning. Morning is also best for staying cool and avoiding the sun, as there is little shade at the bronze monument. There are also fewer people there at night when the monument is illuminated.
