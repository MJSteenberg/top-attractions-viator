Q:How much does it cost to visit Casco Viejo in 2024?
A:

While its free to visit Casco Viejo on your own, you can get more immersed in the history and culture of Panama Citys Old Town on an affordable tour with an expert guide. The most affordable city tours start around US$35 and more extensive tours can range from US$60 to US$240 or more depending on the tours itinerary, amenities, and level of privacy.

Q:Why is Casco Viejo significant in Panama City?
A:

Casco Viejo, also known as Casco Antiguo or Old Quarter, is the historic district of Panama City that was established in 1673. Its now a UNESCO Cultural World Heritage Site. Popular landmarks to visit in Casco Viejo include the Plaza de Francia, the Church of San Jose, and the Canal Museum.

Q:Are there any guided tours available for exploring Casco Viejo?
A:

Yes, there are multiple guided tours available to explore the history, culture, and sights of Casco Viejo. Some tour providers have physical locations in Old Town, while others arrange tours online and provide transportation from your hotelboth in other parts of Panama City and in nearby citiesto Casco Viejo.

Q:Are there any recommended restaurants or cafs in Casco Viejo?
A:

Finca Del Mar is a top-rated restaurant in Casco Viejo near France Square, known for its fresh ceviche and mojitos. La Fishera Seafood is another popular seafood restaurant located in Old Town Plaza Herrera. Diablicos, near Plazoleta Medio Baluarte, is a favorite for traditional Panamanian food.

Q:Where is Casco Viejo located within Panama City?
A:

Casco Viejo is located at the southern tip of Panama City. Bus routes run through the city, and the closest metro station is Estacion 5 de Mayo. The easiest way to visit is either taking a taxi or joining a guided tour of Casco Viejo that has convenient pick-up options from your hotel.

Q:Are there any religious sites or churches in Casco Viejo that are open to visitors?
A:

Yes, one of the most popular churches that tourists visit in Casco Viejo is the Metropolitan Cathedral Basilica of Santa Maria the Ancient, which was originally built in 1796. Located on Independence Square, the church is open throughout the day and is an active place of worship, so please be courteous.

Q:Is Casco Viejo safe for tourists?
A:

Yes, Casco Viejo is generally safe for people visiting Panama Citys historic district. Police patrol the area, and its well-established as a popular tourist area. As always, be mindful when walking alone or at night, or consider booking a guided tour with transportation if you feel uncomfortable.
