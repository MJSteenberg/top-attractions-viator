ChocoMuseo Antigua Tours and Tickets

The Maya were among the earliest civilizations to cultivate cacao for culinary use. At this chocolate-focused museum and shop in Antigua, exhibits detail Guatemala’s long-standing legacy of cacao cultivation as well as documenting chocolate-making processes, such as harvesting, roasting, tempering, and molding.

Q:How much does it cost to visit ChocoMuseo Antigua in 2024?
A:

You can visit the ChocoMuseo to browse the exhibits and purchase chocolate for free, or you can join a paid workshop. Prices for the 2-hour Bean to Bar Workshop are currently USD$25 for adults and USD$20 for children under the age of 12, while the 45-minute Mini Chocolate Workshop is USD$15.

Q:Can I purchase chocolate products at ChocoMuseo?
A:

Yes, the ChocoMuseo also has a small gift shop where you can purchase chocolate, nibs, and beans, plus novelties like mugs, mortars, notebooks, and molinillos. If you join a workshop you’ll make your own chocolate, which you take home at the end.

Q:What other attractions are near ChocoMuseo in Antigua?
A:

The ChocoMuseo is located in the center of Antigua, just across the street from Central Park. San José Cathedral and the Colonial Art Museum are the closest attractions, while the Antigua Brewing Company, Santa Catalina Arch, and the Museo del Hermano Pedro are all within walking distance.

Q:Are there any guided tours available at ChocoMuseo?
A:

No, there aren’t any guided tours of the ChocoMuseo as it is a workshop-based attraction. You can choose between a Bean to Bar Workshop that explores the history and process of chocolate making, or the Mini Chocolate Workshop, which is an expedited version of the main workshop.

Q:Is ChocoMuseo suitable for families with young children?
A:

Yes, ChocoMuseo is a suitable attraction and activity for families with young children. Little kids will enjoy making their own chocolate, while sampling the chocolate is undoubtedly one of the best parts for all.
