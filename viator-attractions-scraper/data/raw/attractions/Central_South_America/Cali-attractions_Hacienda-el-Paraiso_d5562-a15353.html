With its fragrant rose gardens and water features, Hacienda el Paraíso (Paradise House) lives up to its name. Originally built in 1816 as a base for supervision of the region’s sugarcane plantations, the hacienda became famous in the 19th century when it was used as the setting forMaría, a novel by iconic Colombian writer Jorge Isaacs.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Hacienda el Paraíso in 2024?
A:

An admission ticket to Hacienda el Paraíso currently costs 15K pesos and grants you access to all of its rooms and the gardens surrounding it. Day trips from Cali typically combine a visit to the hacienda with a stop at the Museo de la Cana de Azucar (Cali Sugar Cane Museum) and a traditional Colombian lunch; tours start from around US$149.

Q:What are the highlights of Hacienda el Paraíso?
A:

The highlights of Hacienda el Paraíso, which was the setting of Jorge Isaacs' seminal romantic novel María, are its rose garden, its vast grounds, the on-site Hacienda Paradise House Museum, and its traditional whitewashed architecture dating back to the 19th century. You can enjoy views of the sugar cane plantations and lush foothills of the Andes mountains from all parts of the estate.

Q:Can I visit the sites where *María*, Jorge Isaacs' famous novel, was set?
A:

Yes, you can visit many of the rooms that were mentioned in Jorge Isaacs' famous novel, including the study where Edraín gave lessons to María, the living room where the family socialized, the oratory where the priest would deliver mass services, and the kitchen, which houses period pieces consistent with the novel.

Q:What kind of facilities are at Hacienda el Paraíso?
A:

As well as the House Museum, the grounds of Hacienda el Paraíso are home to a gift shop, a café, and a parking lot. You can also stay at the Hotel Piedemonte, which has 24 rooms, natural pools, and a restaurant that serves Colombian specialties.

Q:Are there any other attractions near Hacienda el Paraíso?
A:

Yes, Hacienda el Paraíso is within easy reach of several other interesting attractions. Museo de la Caña de Azúcar is 15 minutes away and has beautiful gardens and historic sugar mills, while Hacienda Piedechinche is another example of a well-preserved estate turned museum.
