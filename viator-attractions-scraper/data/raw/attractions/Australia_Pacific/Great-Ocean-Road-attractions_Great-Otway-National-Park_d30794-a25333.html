Great Otway National Park Tours and Tickets

Located on Victoria’s Great Ocean Road, Great Otway National Park is a lush area of forests, waterfalls, and windswept coastline. Whether you want to hike for an hour or several days, enjoy panoramic views from an historical lighthouse, bike through fern gullies, gaze at picture-perfect waterfalls, or seek out glowworms, there’s a lot to do here.

People Also Ask
Q:What is special about Great Otway National Park?
A:

The Great Otway National Park is rich in biodiversity, from giant redwoods, waterfalls, and fern gullies to coastal regions along the Great Ocean Road. The park offers opportunities for beautiful drives, mountain biking, hiking, and learning about the area’s history at the Cape Otway Lightstation. You might even see koalas.

Q:How long should I stay at Great Otway National Park?
A:

Plan to spend at least 3–4 days in the Great Otway National Park so you can take your time when stopping at waterfalls and ocean viewing areas or when scouting for wildlife. You can camp or stay at hotels in several towns, including Lorne, Apollo Bay, and Forrest.

Q:When is the best time to visit Great Otway National Park?
A:

There’s something to see all year-round at Great Otway National Park, but the best time to visit is during the Aussie summer (December–February). The spring and fall shoulder seasons, beginning in October and ending in April, also offer good weather but with fewer crowds.

Q:Where is the best place to spot koalas at Great Otway National Park?
A:

The best place to see koalas in their natural habitat at Great Otway National Park is near the Kennett River. Find the eucalyptus trees, where you’re more likely to spot a koala among the leaves. Koalas are most active early in the morning or in the evening.

Q:What animals will I find at Great Otway National Park?
A:

Great Otway National Park is home to an array of wildlife, including koalas. Head to Lake Elizabeth to look for the shy platypus. In the early morning and evening, keep your eyes peeled for kangaroos and the smaller hoppers—the wallabies. Birds, both permanent and migratory, also nest in the park.

Q:Is Great Otway National Park worth it?
A:

Yes, the Great Otway National Park is worth it, whether as a day trip (just a few hours from Melbourne) or as part of a more extended vacation. The park’s natural beauty offers opportunities for exploration across adventure levels and ages—it’s great for relaxation seekers, too.
