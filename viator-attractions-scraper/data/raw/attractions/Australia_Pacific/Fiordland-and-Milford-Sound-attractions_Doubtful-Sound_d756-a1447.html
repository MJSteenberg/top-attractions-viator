Doubtful Sound Tours and Tickets

1,088 reviews

While tour buses may flock to neighboring Milford Sound, the wild landscapes of Doubtful Sound are equally enchanting and much less crowded. Framed by looming cliffs and dotted with rocky islets and tumbling waterfalls, this natural fjord is three times longer and has 10 times more surface area than Milford Sound. It is also centrally located at the heart of Fiordland National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Doubtful Sound in 2024?
A:

While its free to explore Doubtful Sound independently, most travelers explore this area on a boat trip. Prices for a full-day trip from Manapouri typically begin at around US$198, with higher prices from Queenstown at around US$258. Overnight cruises are closer to US$499, and helicopter tours are from US$599.

Q:What is Doubtful Sound, and where is it located in New Zealand?
A:

Doubtful Sound is New Zealands deepest fjord, known for its magnificent natural landscapes, including immense cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and abundant wildlife. Located in Fiordland National Park along the remote southwestern coast of the South Island, its about 100 miles (170 kilometers) southwest of Queenstown and just south of Milford Sound.

Q:How do I get to Doubtful Sound from Queenstown or Te Anau?
A:

Doubtful Sound cant be reached by road, so youll need to drive or take the bus to Manapouriabout a 2-hour journey from Queenstown or 20 minutes from Te Anau. From there, cross Lake Manapouri by boat, then drive over the Wilmot Pass to Deep Cove, where fjord cruises depart.

Q:What is the best way to experience Doubtful Sound: cruise or kayak?
A:

Why not try both? Most kayaking tours explore part of the fjord by kayak before returning by boat, affording the best of both worlds. While kayaking does let you get closer to the shore and wildlife, you will need a suitable fitness level and may get wet!

Q:Are there guided tours available for Doubtful Sound?
A:

Yesin fact, the only way to visit the remote Doubtful Sound is by guided tour. Land tours include a boat ride across Lake Manapouri to reach the Sound, after which youll explore the fjord by boat or kayak. Alternatively, scenic flights and helicopter tours afford magnificent aerial views of the fjord.

Q:What wildlife can visitors expect to see in Doubtful Sound?
A:

The striking scenery isnt the only highlight of Doubtful Soundits also a popular destination for wildlife watching. Look out for bottlenose dolphins, New Zealand fur seals, and penguins, including the rare Fiordland crested penguin. If youre lucky, you might also see southern right whales or minke whales.

Q:Are there any hiking trails or walking tracks near Doubtful Sound?
A:

Yes, there are four hiking trails near Doubtful Sound, which range from the easy Brassell Point Nature Walk (1-hour return) to the challenging Old Doubtful Sound Track (3-hour return). All trails leave from Deep Cove at the edge of the fjord, so youll still need to travel across Lake Manapouri and Wilmot Pass.
