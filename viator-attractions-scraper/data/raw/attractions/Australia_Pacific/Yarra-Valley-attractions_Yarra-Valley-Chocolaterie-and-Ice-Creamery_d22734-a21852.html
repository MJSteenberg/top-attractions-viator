Q:When did the Yarra Valley Chocolaterie open?
A:
Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery opened in 2012 by Leanne and Ian Neeland, a married couple who have also opened chocolate shops on Great Ocean Road and in Mornington Peninsula. Although Yarra Valley Chocolaterie has plenty of staff, you may end up meeting one of the owners on your visit.
Q:Do you need reservations to visit Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery?
A:
No, you do not need reservations to visit Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery. Neither the café nor the showroom take reservations. You simply need to show up. If you are short on time, you can view the café menu online ahead of time so you know what to order.
Q:Can you go to Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery on your own?
A:
Yes, you can go to Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery on your own, though you will need a car to get there if you’re coming from Melbourne. The Chocolaterie takes about an hour to reach from Melbourne by car and is a popular stop on Yarra Valley tours.
Q:How much does it cost to visit Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery?
A:
Entry to Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery is free, and bookings are not required. The shop offers free samples as well as tasting sessions (for a small fee), but most people end up buying treats to eat onsite or take home. Kid-friendly chocolate-making classes are also available, for a fee.
Q:What is the best time of year to visit Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery?
A:
If you like ice cream, consider visiting Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery in summer. However, the sweet shop is open daily, throughout the year, making it a great place to visit, no matter the season. They’re open on public holidays, except for Christmas Day.
Q:Is visiting Yarra Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery worth it?
A:
Yes, visiting Valley Chocolaterie & Ice Creamery is worth it. Not only will you have the chance to try all sorts of delicious chocolate confections, but you’ll also be given the opportunity to learn about chocolate and how it’s made. You can even take a chocolate-making class.
