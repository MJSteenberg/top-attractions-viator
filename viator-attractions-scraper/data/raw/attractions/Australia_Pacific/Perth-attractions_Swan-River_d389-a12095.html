Swan River Tours and Tickets

4,253 reviews

The liquid heart of Perth, the Swan River touches many of the city’s neighborhoods on its way to the Indian Ocean. The river passes through the Swan Valley wine region, Perth’s Central Business District and affluent suburbs, and the port city of Fremantle, and there are lots of recreational opportunities on the banks and in the water.

Q:What are the top attractions along the Swan River in Western Australia?
A:
The Swan River runs for 45 miles (72 kilometers), largely through metropolitan Perth. On a river cruise or kayak, you’ll see the Perth city skyline, Kings Park, the iconic Swan Bells, the vineyard-rich Swan Valley, the cliffs of Blackwall Reach, and the golden-hued Point Walter spit.
Q:Where can I book river cruises on the Swan River?
A:
Most cruises along Australia’s Swan River operate largely out of Perth. You can prebook online to secure the date and time that works for you. Cruises on the Swan River pass top attractions like the Bell Tower and Kings Park.
Q:Are there wildlife tours on the Swan River?
A:
While you won’t find wildlife tours on the Swan River like on the Amazon, you will find outfitters focusing on the river's natural side and its flora and fauna. Take a tour that visits Rottnest Island, for example, and you might spot dolphins, pelicans, eagles, black swans, and parrots.
Q:Can I rent a private boat for a cruise on the Swan River?
A:
Yes, you can rent a private boat for a cruise on the Swan River. Multiple Perth-based companies will help you charter a vessel. Get on the water for an afternoon adventure, or opt for a romantic, music-filled nighttime excursion. Some companies will provide a captain, so you can relax.
Q:What is the best time of year for a Swan River cruise?
A:
The best time of year for a Swan River cruise is typically spring (September–November) and autumn (March–May). However, Perth’s weather is as gorgeous as it comes, with mild temps year-round. Be prepared for the rainier days of June through August.
Q:How long is the typical duration of a Swan River cruise?
A:
The typical duration of a Swan River Cruise varies depending on the outfitter and type of excursion, though most last two to three hours. Generally, sightseeing tours are shorter than dinner cruises. And, of course, you can always charter a boat for a half-day or full-day adventure.
