Whitehaven Beach Tours and Tickets

With its powder-white silica sands, gleaming turquoise waters, and fringe of lush rainforest, it’s little surprise that Whitehaven is one of Australia’s most photographed beaches. Stretching for almost 3 miles (5 kilometers) along the coast of Whitsunday Island, it’s a magnificent sight and an idyllic spot for swimming and snorkelling.

Q:How do I get to Whitehaven Beach?
A:

Whitehaven Beach is located on the southeast coast of Whitsunday Island, the largest of the Whitsunday Islands, and can only be reached by boat, seaplane, or helicopter. By boat, it’s about a 1.5-hour journey from Airlie Beach on the mainland, while seaplane and helicopter take about 15 minutes.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit Whitehaven Beach?
A:

The tropical climate of the Whitsundays means you can swim at Whitehaven Beach through all four seasons. Summer (December-February) is the most popular, but it’s also stinger season. Come in early spring (September) instead when there are fewer crowds, warm waters, and opportunities to watch the winter whale migration.

Q:Are there any tours available to Whitehaven Beach?
A:

Yes. Whitehaven Beach is one of the most popular attractions in the Whitsunday Islands, and most travelers visit on a day tour from Hamilton Island or Airlie Beach. Cruise around the Whitsundays, stopping to stroll Whitehaven Beach, admire the views from Hill Inlet Lookout, or take a scenic flight.

Q:What are the highlights of Whitehaven Beach?
A:

Whitehaven Beach is known for its “swirling sands,” a natural phenomenon caused when the white silica sands shelf down into the aquamarine ocean, creating a dramatic visual effect from above. A seaplane or helicopter flight over the beach is a highlight, as are swimming, bushwalking, and beachside camping.

Q:Is camping allowed on Whitehaven Beach? Are there facilities available?
A:

Yes, camping is allowed on Whitehaven Beach, but only at the dedicated bush camping area. The campground is at the south end of the beach and has 10 walk-in tent-only sites with non-flush toilets and a sheltered communal picnic table. Camping permits are required and must be booked in advance.

Q:Can I swim at Whitehaven Beach? Are there any safety precautions I should take?
A:

Yes, you can swim at Whitehaven Beach, and the waters are warm and calm year-round. However, swimming is at your own risk. There’s no lifeguard service or phone reception at the beach, and stingers, sharks, and occasionally crocodiles can be present. Stinger suits are recommended in summer.
