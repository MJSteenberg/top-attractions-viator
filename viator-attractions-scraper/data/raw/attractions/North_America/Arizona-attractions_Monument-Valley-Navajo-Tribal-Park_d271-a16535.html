Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park is the full name for those red-rock landscapes you know so well. Beloved by Hollywood, this sacred area claims almost-eerie rock formations that soar up to 1,000 feet (305 meters) from the dusty desert floor. Under the jurisdiction of the Navajo Nation, you can see some formations from the highway, but to truly explore the area, you’ll need to go deeper.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park in 2025?
A:

As of 2025, the entrance fee for Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park is US$8 per person, while children aged 7 and under enter for free. However, many areas within the park are restricted and only accessible via guided tours, which may incur a fee. Check ahead with the Navajo Nation for the latest pricing.

Q:Can I visit Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park on my own, or do I need a guide?
A:

Yes, you can visit Monument Valley on your own and drive along the 17-mile scenic Valley Drive. You’ll need to take a guided tour led by a Navajo guide if you’d like to access areas beyond the drive and get a more in-depth experience; these tours provide insights into the cultural and spiritual significance of the area and usually travel to spots closed to the general public.

Q:Is Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park the same as Monument Valley?
A:

Monument Valley refers to the broader geographical area on the Arizona-Utah border, famous for its towering red sandstone formations. Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park is a protected area within Monument Valley, managed by the Navajo Nation, where visitors can explore the iconic landscapes, hike on the trails, and learn about Navajo culture.

Q:Do you need a permit for Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park?
A:

A general entrance fee grants access to most of Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park. You’ll either need a special permit or to take a guided tour if you want to go off the main road, camp, hike beyond the Wildcat Trail, or take professional photographs or videos. Check with park authorities for specific permit requirements, depending on your activities.

Q:Are there hiking trails in Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park?
A:

Yes, there are hiking opportunities within the park. The most popular route is the Wildcat Trail, a loop that circles around the West Mitten Butte for 3.2 miles (5.1 km). This is the only self-guided hiking trail in the park; for other hiking experiences, book a Navajo-led guided tour, as much of the land is sacred Navajo territory.
