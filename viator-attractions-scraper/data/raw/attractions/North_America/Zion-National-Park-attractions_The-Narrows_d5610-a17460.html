The Narrows Tours and Tickets

One of Zion National Park’s most famous hikes, The Narrows are the narrowest section of Zion Canyon, with sandstone walls reaching 1,000 feet (305 meters) high and sometimes 20 feet (6 meters) across. The Virgin River flows underfoot for most of this adventurous trek—be prepared to get wet.

Q:How much does it cost to visit The Narrows in 2024?
A:

As of 2024, the entrance fee into Zion National Park—where you’ll find The Narrows—is US$35 per private vehicle, good for seven days. Beyond the park entrance fee, exploring The Narrows is free. However, there are plenty of tours that will make getting the best views far easier, which tend to cost around US$400.

Q:How do I get tickets or permits for The Narrows?
A:

To hike The Narrows in Zion National Park, you don’t need a permit for the bottom-up hike (the standard hike), which starts from the Temple of Sinawava. However, if you're planning the 16-mile top-down hike, whether as a day hike or an overnight trip, you'll need a permit. You can obtain one through recreation.gov.

Q:Do I need a guide for The Narrows hike?
A:

A guide is not required to hike The Narrows, especially for the bottom-up hike, which is the more popular and accessible route. However, if you're not an experienced hiker or if you're attempting the more challenging top-down hike, you should go with a guide. Navigating the river can be difficult, and it’s important to understand weather conditions when water levels can rise quickly.

Q:What is the best time of year to hike The Narrows?
A:

The best time to hike The Narrows is typically in autumn, specifically around September and October. During this period, water levels are usually lower, which means they’re both warmer and safer for hiking—weather tends to be mild and predictable, too. Avoid hiking in winter and spring, as the water can run high, be cold, and surprisingly dangerous.

Q:How long does it take to hike The Narrows?
A:

That depends on which The Narrows hike you choose. If you’re doing the standard bottom-up hike from the Temple of Sinawava, it’s a day hike. Most visitors hike up to Wall Street, which is a round-trip of about 6 miles (8 kilometers) and typically takes 4-6 hours. You can turn around at any point, making it flexible based on your time and stamina.
