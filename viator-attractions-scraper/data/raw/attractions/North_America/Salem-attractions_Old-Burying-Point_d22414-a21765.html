Old Burying Point (Charter Street Cemetery) Tours and Tickets

18,416 reviews
Free admission

Tombstones from the 17th century draw visitors to Salem into the Old Burying Point, also known as the Charter Street Cemetery. This is the final resting place of victims of the infamous Salem witch trials, in addition to other historical figures, including judges from the Salem witch trials, former governors, and even a pilgrim from the Mayflower. This cemetery is a popular stop on spooky nighttime tours of Salem.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Old Burying Point (Charter Street Cemetery) in 2024?
A:

While visiting the Old Burying Point is free, there are multiple tours available to learn more about the history of the cemetery. Most walking tours cost around US$30, whether theyre ghost tours or general historical tours. Some tours, such as self-guided smartphone tours, can start from around US$6 to US$18.

Q:Where is the Old Burying Point Cemetery located?
A:

The Old Burying Point Cemetery is located at 51 Charter Street in Salems historic center. Attractions close by include the Salem Witch Village, Yin Yu Tang, and the Salem Witch Trials Memorial. From Salem train station, walk south down Washington Street then head east on Front Street, which turns into Charter Street.

Q:Can visitors take photographs inside the Old Burying Point Cemetery?
A:

Yes, visitors can take photographs inside the Old Burying Point Cemetery. Many of the gravestones are clear enough to be legible in photographs, but you should stay on the walking path and not wander through the grass to get pictures. The adjacent Salem Witch Trials Memorial is a popular spot for photography, too.

Q:What is the significance of the Salem Witch Trials Memorial next to the cemetery?
A:

Next to the cemetery, the Salem Witch Trials Memorial is dedicated to the 20 victims of the infamous 1692 trials. Each of the granite benches is dedicated to a victim and lists their name, manner of death, and date of death. The memorial also has court transcripts etched onto it.

Q:What should visitors avoid doing while at the Old Burying Point Cemetery?
A:

Salems Old Burying Point is a real cemetery and should be treated with reverence. Visitors are asked to not venture off of the paths or disturb the burial sites, including the headstones; refrain from making too much noise and not leave any trash behind; conducting or performing any type of inappropriate activities, such as seances, is prohibited.
