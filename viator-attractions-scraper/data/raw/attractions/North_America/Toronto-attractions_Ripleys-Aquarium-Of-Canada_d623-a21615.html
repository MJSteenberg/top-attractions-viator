Ripley's Aquarium of Canada Tours and Tickets

In downtown Toronto, Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada houses approximately 16,000 water-bound species from across the world. The displays are arranged by region, and visitors can explore the tropical Rainbow Reef, check out the Dangerous Lagoon shark tank, and learn more about the marine life found in the waters around Canada.

People Also Ask
Q:What are the main exhibits at Ripley's Aquarium of Canada?
A:

The main exhibits at Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada include the Dangerous Lagoon, an underwater tunnel with sharks and rays; the Rainbow Reef, which is home to the aquarium’s most colorful fish; the Canadian Waters exhibit with animals from all bodies of water surrounding Canada; Planet Jellies with its array of jellyfish species; and the Discovery Centre, where you’ll find hands-on exhibits.

Q:How can I get to Ripley's Aquarium of Canada from downtown Toronto?
A:

If you don’t mind a leisurely stroll, then you can get to Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada from downtown Toronto easily on foot—timing depends on your starting point. Alternatively, Union and St. Andrew are the closest subway stations.

Q:Are there any special events or programs at Ripley's Aquarium of Canada?
A:

Yes there are several daily events and programs at Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada, so it’s advisable to check the calendar before visiting. Daily events typically include dive shows in which divers feed stingrays, plus specialist talks focussed on different creatures, such as piranhas, octopus, and sharks.

Q:Are there any interactive exhibits or activities for children at Ripley's Aquarium of Canada?
A:

Yes, there are interactive exhibits and activities for children at Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada. These include the Discovery Centre, which has a touch pool, a pop-up research submarine, and underwater viewing bubbles. Kids can take part in a variety of hands-on activities.

Q:What amenities and facilities are available for visitors at Ripley's Aquarium of Canada?
A:

Amenities and facilities at Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada include an on-site café, a gift shop, vending machines, and a coat check area. There are also wheelchairs available for rent, wheelchair-accessible parking spots, and fully-accessible bathrooms.

Q:How long should I plan to spend at Ripley's Aquarium of Canada?
A:

To fully explore Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada, it’s best to plan at least three hours. You can easily spend over half a day enjoying the various tanks and exhibits, watching talks and dive shows, and taking breaks at the café.
