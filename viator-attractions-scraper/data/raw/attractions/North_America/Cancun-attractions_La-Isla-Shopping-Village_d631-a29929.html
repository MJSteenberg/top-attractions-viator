La Isla Shopping Village Tours and Tickets

Set along Nichupte Lagoon in Cancun’s Hotel Zone, La Isla Shopping Village is lined with boutique shops, waterfront dining, and luxury shopping. Stroll along the winding canals—which create a Venice-like atmosphere—sip cocktails in classy courtyards, and purchase souvenirs.

Q:How much does it cost to visit La Isla Shopping Village in 2024?
A:

It’s free to visit La Isla Shopping Village on your own; however, there are sightseeing tours that include a stop at the marketplace—along with other popular stops in Cancun. Sightseeing tours start at around 1,628 pesos. The mall also offers additional ticketed entertainment options, such as an interactive aquarium.

Q:Which shops are at La Isla Shopping Village in Cancun?
A:

La Isla Shopping Village in Cancun features a variety of shops, ranging from luxury brands to mainstream stores to local boutiques. Here, you’ll find places like Prada, Dior, Zara, H&M, Nike, Adidas, Ray-Ban, and Sephora, plus local shops selling Mexican crafts and souvenirs. It’s a popular destination in Cancun.

Q:Are there any attractions or entertainment options at La Isla Shopping Village?
A:

Yes, La Isla Shopping Village offers attractions and entertainment options beyond shopping. The Interactive Aquarium Cancun is a family-friendly attraction where you can learn about marine life. The Ferris wheel, located in the mall, offers panoramic views of the area, and the Cinemex theater presents movies in English and Spanish.

Q:Does La Isla Shopping Village offer duty-free shopping?
A:

No, La Isla Shopping Village does not offer duty-free shopping, and taxes and duties apply as they would in any retail store. The mall features a range of luxury brands and local stores for high-end items and souvenirs and is a popular spot for tourists looking for branded goods.

Q:How do I get to La Isla Shopping Village from Cancun’s Hotel Zone?
A:

To get to La Isla Shopping Village from the Hotel Zone in Cancun via public transportation, take the R1 or R2 bus, which runs frequently along Boulevard Kukulcan, the main road in the Hotel Zone; La Isla is a designated stop. Taxis are also readily available throughout the Hotel Zone.
