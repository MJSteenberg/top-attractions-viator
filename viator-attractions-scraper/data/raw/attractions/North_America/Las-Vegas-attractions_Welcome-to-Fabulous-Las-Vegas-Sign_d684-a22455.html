Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign Tours and Tickets

Constructed in 1959, the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas sign is an icon from the era of classic Vegas. Listed on the US National Register of Historic Places, the neon landmark welcomes visitors driving into the city from the south on Las Vegas Boulevard, aka the Strip.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign in 2024?
A:

It’s free to visit the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign independently. But, to learn about Sin City—and the sign—consider opting for a guided tour. Short bus tours start at around US$50, while all-day tours with stops at the Hoover Dam and Grand Canyon start at around US$200.

Q:Where is the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign located?
A:

The Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign is located on the southern end of the Strip, about 4 miles (6.4 kilometers) south of the main resort area. The sign is on the median of Las Vegas Boulevard, making it easily accessible for visitors traveling by car or on foot.

Q:What are the best times to visit the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign for photos?
A:

The best times to visit the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign for photos are early morning (around sunrise) for soft, natural lighting or at night when the sign is lit up in neon glow. Weekday mornings are generally the least crowded.

Q:How do I get to the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign from the Strip?
A:

You can walk to the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign in about 20–30 minutes from popular hotels like Mandalay Bay and Luxor on the southern end of the Strip. Alternatively, take the public bus that travels along Las Vegas Boulevard, or opt for a rideshare service or taxi.

Q:Are there any nearby attractions or activities to do after visiting the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign?
A:

Yes, there are attractions and activities near the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign, including the Mandalay Bay Resort and Casino’s Shark Reef Aquarium and the Titanic Artifact Exhibit at the Luxor Hotel and Casino. Just north, the Pinball Hall of Fame houses vintage pinball machines and arcade games.
