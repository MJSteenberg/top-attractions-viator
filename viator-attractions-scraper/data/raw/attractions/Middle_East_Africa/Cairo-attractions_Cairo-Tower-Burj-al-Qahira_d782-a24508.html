Cairo Tower (Burj al-Qahira) Tours and Tickets

Standing 614 feet (187 meters) tall on Gezira Island, the Cairo Tower (Burj al-Qahira) is one of Cairo’s most recognizable landmarks. Completed in 1961, with a striking lattice exterior designed to resemble a lotus flower, the tower is topped with a café, an observation deck, and a revolving restaurant.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Cairo Tower in 2024?
A:

Admission to the Cairo Tower costs EGP 70 for non-Egyptian visitors and EGP 20 for Egyptians. Evening tour packages are also available, including round-trip transportation between your hotel and the tower and dinner. Such tours typically start at around US$65 per person.

Q:How do I get to the Cairo Tower?
A:

Cairo Tower is located on Gezira Island in the Nile River, roughly a mile (1.6 kilometers) from Tahir Square. There are several ways to get there, including taxis and tours that include round-trip transfers. If you prefer public transportation, you can take the metro to the Opera station on line 2.

Q:What is the best time to visit the Cairo Tower for the best views?
A:

While the Cairo Tower is open from morning until well into the evening, the best time to visit is just before sunset. This allows you to take in views by day and by night. Just make sure to arrive early, as lines can get long at this time of day.

Q:Are there restaurants or cafes in the Cairo Tower?
A:

Yes, there is a cafe and a restaurant at the Cairo Tower. The cafe serves snacks and is a good place for a quick treat, while the revolving restaurant is more of a sit-down affair, and you’ll generally need to make reservations if you wish to dine there.

Q:Is the Cairo Tower accessible for people with disabilities?
A:

Yes, the Cairo Tower is partially accessible for people with disabilities. You can take an elevator to the restaurant to enjoy great city views. However, if you wish to visit the tower’s observation deck, you must climb some stairs.
