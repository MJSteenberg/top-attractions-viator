Alabaster Mosque (Mosque of Muhammad Ali) Tours and Tickets

The citadel of Saladin—and the Cairo skyline—is dominated by the Alabaster Mosque (also known as the Mosque of Muhammad Ali). Modeled on classic Turkish architecture, it took 18 years to build the structure (1830-1848), with a complete restoration taking place in the 1930s. It was commissioned by Muhammad Ali, ruler of Egypt from 1805-1849, who lies in the marble tomb inside.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Alabaster Mosque in 2024?
A:

The Alabaster Mosque is free to enter. Tours that visit the mosque and other attractions range in price from US$36 per person (for tours that only include driver service) to upwards of US$100 per person (for tours that include transportation, guide services, and lunch or a felucca ride).

Q:How do I get to the Alabaster Mosque?
A:

The Alabaster Mosque is located in Islamic Cairo. The best way to get there is by taxi or joining a tour that includes round-trip transportation from your hotel. Public transportation is not easy to use.

Q:Can you go to the Alabaster Mosque on your own?
A:

Yes, you can go to the Alabaster Mosque on your own. However, a guide is a better choice, mainly if you aren’t Muslim or unfamiliar with customs. Going with a guide is also a good idea if you want to learn about the mosque’s history and architecture.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit the Alabaster Mosque?
A:

The best time to visit the Alabaster Mosque is in the morning to avoid the afternoon heat in Cairo. However, note that you may need to wait if you visit during prayer times or on a Friday, the holiest day in Islam. Be aware that the mosque closes early during Ramadan.

Q:Are there guided tours available at the Alabaster Mosque?
A:

Yes, guided tours of the Alabaster Mosque are available. Rather than attending the mosque and seeking a guide, you must book your tour in advance. Most tours are private and typically include transportation and visits to other Cairo attractions, such as the Khan el-Khalili Bazaar.
