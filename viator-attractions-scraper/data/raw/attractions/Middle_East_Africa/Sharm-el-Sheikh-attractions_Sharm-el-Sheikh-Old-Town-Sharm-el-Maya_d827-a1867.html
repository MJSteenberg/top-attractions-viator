Q:What is the best time of day to visit Sharm el Sheikh Old Town?
A:

Late afternoon or evening are great times to visit Sharm el Sheikh Old Town, as all the shops in the Old Market are open, and the scorching summer daytime temperatures have eased. The cafÃ©s and restaurants generally have more atmosphere in the evening than at lunch.

Q:What should I wear when visiting Sharm el Sheikh Old Town?
A:

Sharm el Sheikh Old Town is home to two mosques, the Al Sahaba Mosque behind the Old Market. Respectful dress is important, particularly for women. Wear loose clothing covering shoulders, upper arms, chest, belly, and legs. Proper attire may deflect attention and reduce the risk of sexual harassment.

Q:Are there any safety concerns about Sharm el Sheikh Old Town?
A:

Yes. Sexual harassment can be an issue in Sharm el Sheikh Old Town, particularly for solo women. Like many popular tourist destinations, the Old Market can attract pickpockets and scammers: Leave high-value items in the hotel safe, check your change carefully, and never change money on the street.
