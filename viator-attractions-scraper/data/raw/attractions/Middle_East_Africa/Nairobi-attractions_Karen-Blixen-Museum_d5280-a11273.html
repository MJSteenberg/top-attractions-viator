Karen Blixen Museum Tours and Tickets

451 reviews

The Danish author ofOut of Africa lived in a coffee plantation farmhouse at the edge of Kenya’s beautiful Ngong Hills, where a small museum now celebrates her life and work. Since much of the original furniture has been preserved, it’s a fascinating glimpse into a colonial-era home and an interesting stop even if you haven’t read Blixen’s books.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Karen Blixen Museum in 2024?
A:

In 2024, Karen Blixen Museum admission will cost KHS 200 for adult Kenyan residents and KSH 100 for kids. For non-residents, the cost will be US$12 for adults and US$6 for children. Ticket costs are typically included in the price of tours, but it's wise to check ahead to be sure.

Q:What can I see at the Karen Blixen Museum?
A:

As the name suggests, the museum houses exhibits and ephemera related to the life of Danish author Karen Blixen, famous for Out of Africa. It also houses some original furniture and has lovely gardens, making it a good place for a stroll.

Q:How do I get to the Karen Blixen Museum from Nairobi?
A:

The museum is on Karen Road, in the southwestern part of Nairobi. Taxis will take you here, but many people opt to visit as part of a tour instead. If you’d rather take public transportation, you can also take Matatu Route 24 to the front of the museum.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit the Karen Blixen Museum?
A:

The best time of day to visit the Karen Blixen Museum is in the morning, particularly if you want to avoid crowds. Try to time your visit for a time when there's not too much rain or cloud cover so that you can also enjoy the gardens.

Q:Are there guided tours of the Karen Blixen Museum?
A:

Yes, there are guided tours of the Karen Blixen Museum, and half-hour-long docent-led tours are part of the experience. Many people visit the museum on a guided tour, many of which include stops at nearby attractions, notably the Elephant Orphanage, the Giraffe Centre, and even Nairobi National Park.
