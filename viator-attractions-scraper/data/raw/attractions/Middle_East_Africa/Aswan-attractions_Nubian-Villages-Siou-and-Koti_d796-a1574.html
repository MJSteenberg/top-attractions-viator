Nubian Villages (Siou and Koti) Tours and Tickets

Narrow lanes are lined with gardens and brightly painted houses in the villages of Siou and Koti on Elephantine Island. Trails link the two communities, where Arabic blends with the sounds of the Nubian language. In between the colorful homes, find locals offering souvenirs, henna tattoos, mint tea, and photo ops with captive crocodiles.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Nubian Villages (Siou and Koti) in 2024?
A:

There are no specific fees to visit the Nubian Villages of Siou and Koti, but most people visit on guided tours. Tours range slightly, but you can expect to pay anywhere from US$20 to US$60 per person for most shorter tours.

Q:What do you see on a tour of the Nubian Villages?
A:

Most tours of the Nubian Villages include a walk around the village to view the colorful homes, while some itineraries may offer the chance to meet within members of the Nubian community. Round-trip transportation to Elephantine Island from Aswan’s East Bank is generally included, while some tours might feature a felucca ride instead.

Q:Can you go to the Nubian Villages on your own?
A:

Yes, you can reach Elephantine Island independently by private boat or public ferry. While visiting with a guide is not a requirement, anyone who is interested in Nubian culture or history, or is eager to visit a local household and interact with a Nubian family, should consider booking a tour.

Q:Can you take photos in the Nubian Villages?
A:

Yes, you can take photos in the Nubian villages, which are exceptionally photogenic. Do be aware that while Elephantine Island’s brightly-painted houses are beautiful, they are real homes and not tourist attractions: remember that you are in a tight-knit community space, be mindful of everyone’s right to privacy, and ask permission before taking photographs.

Q:Is visiting the Nubian Villages worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting the Nubian Villages is worth it. The homes are a stunning sight to behold and laid-back Elephantine Island provides a relief from the bustle of downtown Aswan. Nubian communities are Indigenous to the Nile River region with a distinct history and culture of their own, so every visitor should take the time to learn about them.
