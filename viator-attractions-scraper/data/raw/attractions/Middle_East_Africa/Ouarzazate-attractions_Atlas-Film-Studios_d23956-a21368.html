Atlas Film Studios Tours and Tickets

On the edge of the Sahara Desert in Ouarzazate, Atlas Film Studios is not only the center of Morocco’s film industry, but the largest film studio in the world. Founded in the early 1980s, the studios boast an impressive pedigree, having hosted iconic film sets such asGladiator andStar Wars, as well as scenes fromGame of Thrones.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Atlas Film Studios in 2025?
A:

You can only visit the Atlas Film Studios with a guided tour, which costs 80 dirhams per person. There’s an additional 30 dirham fee to visit the Kingdom of Heaven movie set and museum. Expect to tip your guide around 20%. Children under the age of 5 enter for free.

Q:What is Atlas Film Studios in Morocco?
A:

Atlas Film Studios is Morocco’s leading film studio, located in Ouarzazate in the heart of the Saharan desert. Its huge outdoor film sets and magnificent desert backdrop have been used to film many famous movies and TV shows, including Gladiator, Lawrence of Arabia, The Mummy, and Game of Thrones.

Q:What are the opening hours for Atlas Studios?
A:

Atlas Studios is open daily year-round from 8:30am until sunset. Tours typically run every half-hour, depending on the demand, and there is no need to book in advance. Note that certain sets and locations may be off-limits during active filming periods, but tours still run.

Q:What is the best way to get to Atlas Studios from Marrakech?
A:

Atlas Studios is located in the Sahara desert, about a 3-hour drive southeast of Marrakech. There’s no public transport, so you’ll need to join a tour, rent a car, or hire a private driver. Most tours combine a visit with the nearby UNESCO–listed site of Kasbah of Aït Ben Haddou (Ksar of Ait Benhaddou).

Q:What famous movies and shows were filmed at Atlas Studios?
A:

Many legendary movies and modern blockbusters were filmed at Atlas Studios, and tours are full of fun facts about familiar films. Among the most famous are Gladiator, The Jewel of the Nile, Kingdom of Heaven, The Hills Have Eyes, Game of Thrones, and The Mummy.
