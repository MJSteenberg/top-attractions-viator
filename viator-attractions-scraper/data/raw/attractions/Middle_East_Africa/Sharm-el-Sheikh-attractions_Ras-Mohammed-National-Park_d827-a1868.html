Q:How much does it cost to visit Ras Mohammed National Park in 2024?
A:

Official park fees to enter Ras Mohammed National Park vary per nationality. Egyptian citizens can pay as little as EGP 25 (US$0.50), while foreign citizens can be charged as much as EGP 100 (US$2). Day trip organizers often include this fee in their prices, or otherwise ask guests to pay in cash on the boat.

Q:What are the best snorkeling and diving spots in Ras Mohammed National Park?
A:

Top snorkeling and diving spots abound Ras Mohammed National Park include Anemone City and Jackfish Alley, which are both within swimming distance of the shore and popular spots for snorkelers. The quieter site of Ras Ghozlani is as popular for diving as snorkeling, with a diverse array of reef fauna.

Q:What wildlife can I expect to see in Ras Mohammed National Park?
A:

Hammerhead sharks, dolphins, turtles, and manta rays among the bigger marine-based sights at Ras Mohammed National Park, while hundreds of species of coral provide homes for schools of tropical fish such as barracuda, angelfish, and puffer fish. Migratory birds also abound, and species, such as storks and spoonbills, are frequently spotted.

Q:Can I take a day trip to Ras Mohammed National Park from Sharm el Sheik?
A:

Yes, Sharm el Sheik is an easy departure point for day trips to Ras Mohammed National Park. As a tourist hub, the town has plenty of boat operators and guides who work with hotels and tour providers. There are niche options for snorkeling or diving, or excursions that visit White Island, too.
