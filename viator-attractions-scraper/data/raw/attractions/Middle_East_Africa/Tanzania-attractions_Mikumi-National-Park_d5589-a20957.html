Mikumi National Park Tours and Tickets

Among the largest of Tanzania's many national parks, Mikumi National Park is an excellent place for a safari, owing to its size and proximity to Dar es Salaam. All sorts of creatures live here, including elands, wildebeest, zebras, giraffes, lions, baboons, and over 400 bird species.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Mikumi National Park in 2025?
A:

In 2025, the entrance fees for Mikumi National Park are US$30 for adults 16 and older and US$15 for children ages 5 to 15 (for overseas residents). Tanzanians and residents of East Africa pay reduced rates. All children under 5 visit for free.

Q:What is the best month to visit Mikumi National Park?
A:

September is the best month to visit Mikumi National Park. It’s during the dry season (June to October), and you’ll get the chance to see wildlife with fewer crowds, as many people visit from June through August due to school holidays in Europe and North America.

Q:What animals can you see at Mikumi National Park?
A:

You can see all sorts of animals at Mikumi National Park, but it's best to visit without specific expectations. Some animals that frequent the park include zebras, giraffes, wildebeests, elands, lions, and baboons. There are also more than 400 bird species found in the park.

Q:How far is Mikumi National Park from Dar es Salaam?
A:

Mikumi National Park is about 224 miles (361 kilometers) from Dar es Salaam. The drive typically takes about five to six hours, depending on traffic and road conditions. Safari Air Link also offers flights to the park from Dar es Salaam that take under an hour.

Q:Are there guided safari tours in Mikumi National Park?
A:

Yes, guided safari tours visit Mikumi National Park. Many tours depart from Dar es Salaam, but you also can find options from nearby Morogoro—and even from Zanzibar. Some multiday safaris also visit the Selous Game Reserve and Ruaha National Park.
