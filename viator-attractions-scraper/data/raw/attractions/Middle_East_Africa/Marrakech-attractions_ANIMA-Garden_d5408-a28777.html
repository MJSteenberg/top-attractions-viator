ANIMA Garden Tours and Tickets

ANIMA Garden, a fantasy landscape of exotic plants and quirky installations framed by the Atlas Mountains, is the brainchild of Austrian multimedia artist André Heller. Besides over 250 different plant species, works by the likes of Keith Haring and Pablo Picasso deck the 5-acre (2-hectare) grounds. There’s also a museum and a café.

Q:How much does it cost to visit ANIMA Garden in 2024?
A:

ANIMA Garden admission price varies. Adult tickets cost around 140 Moroccan dirham for foreigners and 60 dirham for nationals. Children ages 12–16 are half-price, and younger kids are free. You also can join a tour with transportation from Marrakech. Tours generally start around 394 dirham (garden admission not always included).

Q:When is the best time to visit ANIMA Garden?
A:

The ANIMA Garden is open all year long except during Eid al-Fitr and the surrounding holiday period. Generally, spring and fall are the most temperate and driest months and best for visiting the garden—and the rest of Morocco, too.

Q:How much time should I plan for a visit to ANIMA Garden?
A:

Plan to spend a few hours visiting ANIMA garden and its sculptures, especially in nice weather. You can grab a bite to eat or drink at Café Paul Bowles, where you can get tagines, cakes, coffee, and more. The rooftop terrace offers views of Mount Toubkal, which often is snowcapped.

Q:How do I get to the ANIMA Garden from Marrakech?
A:

It’s about a 45-minute drive to the ANIMA Garden from Marrakech. You can hire a car and driver, join a tour, or negotiate a taxi to drive you there (and wait to take you back). Alternatively, hop on the twice daily shuttles to/from Marrakech in the morning or afternoon.

Q:Is ANIMA Garden good for kids?
A:

Yes, the ANIMA Garden is good for kids. There is plenty of space for kids to run around, search for the next sculpture, and explore the different gardens. Much of the garden is shaded from the sun, too. Children under age 12 are free, and those ages 12–16 pay half-price.
