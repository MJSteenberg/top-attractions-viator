The two Giftun Islands—Giftun Kebir and Giftun Sughayer—are some of the closest to Egypt’s resort town of Hurghada and comprise part of a marine reserve in the Red Sea with spectacular coral reefs and drop-offs teeming with life. Day-trippers come for snorkeling, diving, and sunbathing on the pair of islands’ pristine, protected beaches.

People Also Ask
Q:What are the Giftun Islands and where are they located?
A:
The Giftun Islands are two islands in the Red Sea, around 45 minutes by boat from the coast of Hurghada in Egypt. The islands—Giftun Kebir and Giftun Sughayer—sit within a marine reserve, which ensures the protection of the abundant coral and marine life.
Q:How do I get to the Giftun Islands from Hurghada?
A:
The most hassle-free route to the Giftun Islands is to book an excursion from Hurghada. Most visitors join boat day trips, which include at least a couple of hours on the islands, along with stops to swim and snorkel. Private boat charters are also available.
Q:What activities can I do on the Giftun Islands?
A:
Activities in the Giftun Island are mainly based around its pristine beaches. The islands are renowned for snorkeling and scuba diving, with an abundance of colorful marine life all around. Water sports, such as banana boat rides, are also popular.
Q:What marine life can I expect to see at the Giftun Islands?
A:
The water around the Giftun Islands is rich with sea life and biodiversity. Common sights for snorkelers and scuba divers include parrotfish, butterflyfish, and clownfish. Dolphins are often spotted in the area, along with stingrays, barracuda, and turtles.
Q:What should I bring with me for a day trip to the Giftun Islands?
A:
Many tour operators will provide you with a list of what to bring, depending on the scheduled activities. Typical beach essentials are advisable, such as sunscreen, hats, swimwear, towels, and water. A light umbrella can be a good idea if you prefer some shade on the beach.
Q:Can I stay overnight on the Giftun Islands?
A:
The Giftun Islands are part of a protected marine area and overnight tourism stays aren’t permitted. If you want to stay as close to the islands as possible, consider hotels around Hurghada center or El Gouna, which both offer easy access to the port.
