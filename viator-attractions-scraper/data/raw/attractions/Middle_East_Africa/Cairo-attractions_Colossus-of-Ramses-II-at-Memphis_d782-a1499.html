Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis Tours and Tickets

The Colossus of Ramses II is the showpiece sight at the Memphis Museum, located in Mit Rahinah, just south of Cairo. The open-air museum stands on the site of Memphis, a onetime capital of ancient Egypt, and displays the enormous reclining statue of Ramses inside a 2-story hall, together with a large number of other sculptures throughout its grounds.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis in 2024?
A:

The Colossus of Ramses II is within the Memphis Museum complex. To see it, you must pay museum admission (EGP 10 for Egyptians and EGP 150 for foreigners, with half off for students). Transportation packages from Cairo that visit Memphis, the Pyramids, and Saqqara start at around US$30.

Q:How do I get to the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis?
A:

The Colossus of Ramses II is located at the Memphis Museum, about a 45-minute drive from Giza or an hour's drive from downtown Cairo. To get there, either take a taxi or join a tour. Tours typically visit other stops in the area, including Saqqara and Dashur.

Q:What are the main attractions near the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis?
A:

The main attractions near the Colossus of Ramses II at the Memphis Open-Air Museum include ancient statues and archaeological remains from what was once the capital of Egypt. Many of the area’s other key attractions are at the ancient burial site at Saqqara, home to the Step Pyramid of Djoser.

Q:Are there guided tours of the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis available?
A:

Yes, there are guided tours of the Colossus of Ramses at Memphis. Most tours depart from Cairo and include guide service, round-trip transportation, and visits to other historic attractions such as Saqqara and the Giza Pyramids. Many tours include lunch and admission tickets. Most tours are private.

Q:Is the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis accessible for people with disabilities?
A:

Yes, the Colossus of Ramses II at Memphis is accessible to people with disabilities. The gravel paths at the Memphis Museum, where the Colossus is located, are fairly smooth. Check ahead with your guide for additional information and practicalities.
