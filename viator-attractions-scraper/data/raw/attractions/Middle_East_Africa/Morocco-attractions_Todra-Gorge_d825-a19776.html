Todra Gorge (Todgha Gorge) Tours and Tickets

If you want a taste of Morocco's natural beauty, the Todra Gorge won't disappoint. This red-rock canyon is a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing, though many visitors just drop by for a quick glimpse of the area's towering rock formations on their way to the sand dunes of the Merzouga Desert.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Todra Gorge in 2024?
A:

Hiking tours of the Todra Gorge range from US$50 for just a guide to US$65 for a 4WD adventure. Tours of the Todra Gorge are often multi-day and combine visits to Dades Gorge and the Merzouga Desert, which cost up to US$300 per person, depending on the duration and what is included.

Q:When is the best time to visit the Todra Gorge?
A:

The best times to visit the Todra Gorge are during spring and fall when the temperatures are best for hiking and climbing. Winter is too cold and summer is too hot. No matter what season you visit the gorge, drink plenty of water as it has an arid climate.

Q:How much time should I spend in the Todra Gorge?
A:

To get the full experience of the Todra Gorge, consider going for a half-day hike when you arrive, and then spending the night in a guest house. These accommodations are often run by Berber staff, who show you their culture with song, drumming, and clapping after a local dinner.

Q:Is the Todra Gorge good for kids?
A:

Yes, the Todra Gorge is good for kids, especially if they like to hike and climb. There are more moderate routes that are good for all levels and ages, and your guide can point you in the right direction. Kids also like learning about Berber culture through music and hospitality.

Q:Is the Todra Gorge worth it?
A:

Yes, the Todra Gorge is worth it. The scenery is remarkable and contrasts with the landscapes you’ll find in other parts of Marrakech, like the desert, dense cities, and coastal areas. There are plenty of trails to hike or bike, which are welcome after long and unavoidable drives.
