Souk el Had Tours and Tickets

Also written Souq al Had, Souk el Had is Agadir’s main market, a cavernous warehouse of around 6,000 stalls selling everything from spices, fruit, and vegetables through to clothes, perfume, carpets, pottery, shisha pipes, and electronics. Tailors around the market can alter garments or make them to your specifications.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Souk el Had in 2024?
A:

While Souk el Had is free to visit independently, many travelers opt to explore with a guide. Group walking tours of Agadir with a local guide start at just 110 Moroccan dirham and afford insight into the souks, the traditional items and foods on sale, and shopping and haggling etiquette.

Q:What can I expect to find at Souk el Had?
A:

Souk El Had is Morocco’s largest urban market, with some 6,000 shops and stalls, and you can expect to find just about everything on sale. Clothing, electronics, and homeware are sold here, as well as traditional handicrafts, Moroccan specialties like argan oil and Amlou, and a myriad of spices.

Q:When is the best time to visit Souk el Had?
A:

The market operates daily Tuesday–Sunday, and while there are no official opening hours, most stalls are open from 6am to 8:30pm. Come on a weekday morning for a quieter shopping experience with fewer crowds. Or, opt to arrive on a Sunday—the busiest day—to soak up the atmosphere.

Q:How should I dress for a visit to Souk el Had?
A:

Morocco is a Muslim country, so it’s respectful to adhere to local customs and wear modest clothing. This generally means covering your upper arms and legs. Opt for lightweight clothing, as it can get hot in the souks, and wear comfy shoes, as you’ll be doing plenty of walking.

Q:Is haggling expected at Souk el Had, and how should I approach it?
A:

Yes, haggling is all part of the shopping experience in Morocco! It’s useful to have an idea of prices beforehand—this is where a local guide can help. Otherwise, start by offering 50 percent of the starting price. Generally, it’s best only to start negotiations if you’re genuinely interested in buying.
