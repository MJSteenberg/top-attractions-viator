Q:How much does it cost to visit Amman Citadel in 2024?
A:

It costs international visitors 3 Jordanian dinars to visit the Amman Citadel. Jordanian passport holders can expect to pay a discounted rate of 0.25 Jordanian dinar. The landmark is often included on Amman city tours, for which tourists can expect to pay anywhere from 40 to 60 Jordanian dinars.

Q:What is the best month to visit Amman Citadel?
A:

Aim for spring or fall shoulder seasons for the most pleasant weather conditions. If you visit in summer, bring plenty of water and head to the Citadel at opening hours to avoid midday heat. Winter in Amman often brings rain, so it isn't ideal for sightseeing.

Q:What is Amman Citadel known for?
A:

Located on a hilltop, the Citadel shows the historical layers behind Amman, from the remains of an ancient temple dedicated to the Roman god Hercules to a Byzantine church and Umayyad Palace complex. Today, the site is one of the capital's most popular tourist destinations, appreciated for its panoramic views.

Q:How much time should I spend in Amman Citadel?
A:

That depends. If you’re short on time, around one to two hours will allow you to see the highlights. But if you’re interested in visiting the on-site archaeology museum or gaining a deeper understanding of Jordan’s ancient history, consider allotting at least three hours and coming with a guide.

Q:Is visiting Amman Citadel worth it?
A:

Yes, definitely. Amman Citadel is often cited as a favorite tourist attraction in the capital. Unlike other parts of Jordan, Amman isn’t brimming with historical sights, so you shouldn’t pass through the city without stopping here. Bring your camera or smartphone, as the photo opportunities are endless.
