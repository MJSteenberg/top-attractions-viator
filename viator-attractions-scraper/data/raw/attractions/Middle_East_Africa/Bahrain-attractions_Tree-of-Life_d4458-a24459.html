Tree of Life (Shajarat-al-Hayat) Tours and Tickets

577 reviews
Free admission

Over 400 years old, Bahrain’s mystical Tree of Life (Shajarat-al-Hayat) is one of the country’s top attractions. No one really knows how this ancient mesquite is still alive, as the area receives very little rainfall and there is no known source of water, though some believe that the tree was part of the Biblical Garden of Eden and has magical powers.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Tree of Life in 2025?
A:

There is no entrance fee to visit the Tree of Life. Shared sightseeing tours start from US$92 and tend to visit the tree in combination with other Bahrain highlights, such as the UNESCO-listed Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain) and the enormous Al-Fateh Grand Mosque.

Q:Is the Tree of Life worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting the Tree of Life is worth it—the ancient Mesquite tree is a scientific marvel and survives in the inhospitable desert setting against all odds. The tree is more than 500 years old and some believe that it stands in the site of the Christian Garden of Eden.

Q:Are there any facilities near the Tree of Life?
A:

There are some basic facilities near the Tree of Life in Bahrain, such as restrooms and a parking lot. Signage is minimal, so it’s best to either read up on the tree ahead of your visit or join a guided tour.

Q:Are there any nearby attractions or landmarks to visit after seeing the Tree of Life?
A:

Yes, there are several landmarks within easy reach of the Tree of Life. Must-see sights include Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain), a UNESCO World Heritage Site dating back more than 4,000 years, and the Royal Camel Farm, where you can interact with the humped creatures.

Q:What should you bring when visiting the Tree of Life, especially in the desert climate?
A:

Bring sun protection and water when visiting the Tree of Life, as the area surrounding the tree is completely barren and exposed to the elements. Wear closed-toe shoes to protect your feet from the hot sand. The tree is the only attraction in the vicinity, so most visitors only spend 10–20 minutes here.
