Abu Serga (Church of St. Sergius and Bacchus) Tours and Tickets

Abu Serga, or the Church of St. Sergius and Bacchus, competes with the Hanging Church for the title of Coptic Cairo’s oldest church. Although it’s been rebuilt many times, its pillars date back to the fourth century, and much of the structure is medieval. It’s named for a pair of martyred Roman soldiers, St. Sergius (Abu Serga) and St. Bacchus.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Abu Serga in 2024?
A:

Admission to Abu Serga is free. Tours that visit the highlights of Coptic and Islamic, including Abu Serga and other key attractions, can cost as little as US$45 (for tours that include all transportation and guide service) to US$75 or more (a typical range for tours that include lunch).

Q:How do I get to Abu Serga?
A:

Abu Serga is located in Coptic Cairo and is a major stop on tours that explore this historic part of the Egyptian capital. If you want to visit on your own, you can take line 1 of the metro to the Mar Girgis Station, a few minutes walk away, or take a taxi.

Q:Is there a dress code at Abu Serga?
A:

Yes, there is an expected dress code at Abu Serga. As this is a place of worship for members of the Coptic Christian community, please dress modestly with clothes that cover your legs and arms. This applies to both men and women. Note that headscarves are not required.

Q:Is visiting Abu Serga worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting Abu Serga is worth it for its aesthetic value alone. It’s a beautiful old church with gorgeous interior architecture and intricate adornment. Some believe that it marks the spot where Mary, Joseph, and Jesus hid from Herod during the Massacre of the Innocents.

Q:Is Abu Serga accessible for people with disabilities?
A:

Sort of. While wheelchair users can see the church from the outside, the interior of Abu Serga is not wheelchair accessible, as a few steps lead down into the church. However, some people with compromised mobility who can still walk may be able to make it inside.
