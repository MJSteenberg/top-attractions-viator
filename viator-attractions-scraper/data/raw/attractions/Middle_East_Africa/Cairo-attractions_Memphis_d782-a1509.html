Q:How much does it cost to visit Memphis, Egypt in 2024?
A:

It costs 80 Egyptian pounds to visit the key attraction in Memphis—the Mit Rahina Open-Air Museum—for foreigners, but many people pay for package tours that include round-trip transportation from Cairo hotels. Many tours also visit Saqqara and the Pyramids of Giza. These typically cost US$50 to US$60.

Q:What is Memphis in Egypt and why is it significant?
A:

Memphis is an ancient city that was the capital of Egypt during the reign of the Old Kingdom (starting around 3000 BC), a period that saw much of Ancient Egypt’s most impressive architectural innovation. Today, the archaeological site serves as an open-air museum and draws in plenty of visitors interested in history.

Q:Where is Memphis located in Egypt and how do I get there?
A:

Memphis is situated in what is now the village of Mit Rahina, about 18 miles (29 kilometers) from downtown Cairo. There is bus service between Cairo and Mit Rahina, though it’s easier to go by taxi or join a guided tour that includes round-trip transportation from your hotel in Cairo or Giza.

Q:What are the must-see attractions within the Memphis archaeological site?
A:

The Memphis Archaeological Site is full of things to see, from statues to temples. One of the most famous attractions is the Alabaster Sphinx, which was carved from a single slab of alabaster back in around 1550 BC. The site also has numerous temples dedicated to Hathor, Rameses II, and Ptah.

Q:When is the best time of day to visit the Memphis open-air museum?
A:

The Memphis open-air museum is typically open daily from morning until late afternoon, though timings can vary, especially during Ramadan—when the site may close early—or on certain public holidays. It’s a good idea to come as early as you can to avoid crowds and the heat of the day.

Q:Can I explore Memphis independently, or is it recommended to join a tour?
A:

Yes, you can explore Memphis independently, but you should consider joining a tour. Going with a guide is ideal if you want to learn about the history of the site. Most tours from Cairo include round-trip transportation from your hotel, making them a convenient alternative to going alone.

Q:What other ancient sites or attractions are located near Memphis that are worth visiting?
A:

Ancient attractions near Memphis, Egypt include Saqqara (Sakkara)—the ancient burial complex back when Memphis was the Egyptian capital—and Dahshur, an ancient complex housing some of the oldest pyramids in Egypt (the Bent Pyramid and the Red Pyramid). Tours to Memphis from Cairo typically stop at both Saqqara and Dahshur, and some also visit the Giza Pyramids.
