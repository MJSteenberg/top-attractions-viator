Q:How much does it cost to visit Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo)?
A:

Marco Polo's Home in Venice is a private building—you can walk by to see a plaque announcing that the explorer once lived there. There are, however, affordable tours that can help you easily reach the building. Walking tours start at around US$30 while Gondola ride combos start at around US$60.

Q:What can I see and do at Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo)?
A:

Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo) lies in a quiet corner of Venice near the Marco Polo Bridge—since it's not a museum, you can’t do much besides check out the marble plaque announcing that the explorer once lived there. However, searching for the building through alleys is a quintessential Venice experience.

Q:Is there a guided tour available for Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo)?
A:

Since Marco Polo's Home is a private building—and not a museum—no guided dedicated tours of the home are available. However, given that Marco Polo is one of Venice’s most famous historical figures, many private walking and history tours pass by to point out the historical plaque on the home's wall.

Q:Are there any attractions near Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo)?
A:

Yes, there are attractions near Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo). Among them are the famous Rialto Bridge; St. Mark’s Square, widely considered the heart of Venice; the Church of Santa Maria dei Miracoli; the Grand Canal; the Rialto Market; and Ca' d'Oro, a prestigious Gothic palace.

Q:How do I get to Marco Polo's Home (Casa di Marco Polo) from St. Mark’s Square?
A:

You can reach Marco Polo's Home from St. Mark’s Square in about 10 minutes on foot. Walk north through Merceria Street, then turn right toward Piscina S. Zulian and cross the Malvasia Bridge. Continue northwest toward the Marco Polo Bridge—the home is on Corte Seconda del Milion.
