Seville Cathedral (Catedral de Santa María de la Sede) Tours and Tickets

The world’s largest Gothic cathedral, built atop the remains of a mosque, the Seville Cathedral (Catedral de Santa Maria de la Sede) features a spectacular gold altarpiece in its main altar depicting 36 scenes from the life of Christ, as well as the tomb of Christopher Columbus, works by Goya and Murillo, and the dramatic Giralda Tower.

People Also Ask
Q:Do you need tickets for Seville Cathedral?
A:
Yes, you need an entry ticket to admire the stunning architecture of Seville Cathedral and its rich history from the interior. You can purchase the tickets at the door or online before visiting to save time standing in the long queue at this UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Q:Is there a dress code for Seville Cathedral?
A:
It is strongly advised to dress appropriately and respectfully out of consideration for the religious significance of the cathedral. Avoid wearing revealing clothing, shorts, or tank tops for both men and women, as a sign of respect for the local culture and traditions.
Q:How much time do you need at Seville Cathedral?
A:
To fully appreciate all the art and architecture on display, most visitors allot anywhere from 1-2 hours to explore every nook and cranny of Seville Cathedral. Guided or audio tours also last about 1 hour, with some extra time to explore afterward.
Q:What is the best time of day to go to Seville Cathedral?
A:
The early morning boasts cool temperatures with a quieter atmosphere if you want to beat the crowds that fill the cathedral in the middle of the day. Evening hours are also cooler while the sun is setting, and the colors of the cathedral are illuminated with candles and lights.
Q:Is Seville Cathedral accessible?
A:
Yes, Seville Cathedral is wheelchair-friendly and equipped with ramps and handrails to ensure that visitors of all abilities can enjoy this stunning structure. While the main hall is flat, ramps also allow access to other important tombs and relics.
Q:Is Seville Cathedral worth it?
A:
If you love history, architecture, or art, Seville Cathedral is a must-see destination and UNESCO World Heritage Site in Spain. As one of the largest churches in the world, the landmark is a stunning feat of design and boasts an impressive collection of sacred artwork that spans centuries.
