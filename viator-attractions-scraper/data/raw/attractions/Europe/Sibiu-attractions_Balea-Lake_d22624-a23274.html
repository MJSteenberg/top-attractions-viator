Nestled among Romania’s lush Fagaras Mountains at 6,560 feet (2,000 meters) above sea level, Bâlea Lake (Lacul Bâlea) is one of the country’s most scenic bodies of water. While the lake water is perennially chilly for swimming, Bâlea Lake offers a wealth of things to do, which range from scenic hikes to snow sledding.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Balea Lake in 2024?
A:

You can visit Balea Lake for free, but getting there isn't free. Tickets for the Balea Lake cable car cost 50 lei one way for adults and 30 lei for ages 2–12. Skip the hassle of renting a car and driving and, instead, visit on a tour (starting around €120).

Q:What activities can I do at Balea Lake?
A:

In addition to admiring the views of Balea Lake, you can enjoy outdoor activities. Hiking trails lead from the lake to viewpoints with panoramic vistas of the Fagaras Mountains. They also connect to other alpine lakes and waterfalls. In summer, bold visitors can plunge into the lake to cool off.

Q:Is Balea Lake accessible during the winter?
A:

Yes, Balea Lake is accessible in winter, as the area is a popular ski resort. However, road access to Balea Lake is closed seasonally due to poor road conditions, meaning the only way to reach Balea Lake in winter is via cable car from the north side.

Q:Is there any accommodation near Balea Lake?
A:

Yes, accommodations at Balea Lake are available for those who want to spend longer in the Fagaras Mountains and other parts of the Transfagarasan Highway. Options include two mountain chalets overlooking the lake and the seasonal Hotel of Ice, where visitors can stay in rooms carved of ice in winter.

Q:What are the nearby attractions to visit after Balea Lake?
A:

The mountains around Balea Lake are home to attractions you can visit before or after the lake. They include Balea Waterfall near the bottom cable car station, Capra Lake (just over the ridge from Balea Lake), and Capra Waterfall on the southern descent along the Transfagarasan Highway.
