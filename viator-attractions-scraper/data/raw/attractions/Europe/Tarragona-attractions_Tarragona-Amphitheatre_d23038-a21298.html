Tarragona Amphitheatre (Amfiteatre de Tarragona) Tours and Tickets

If there’s one sight that will successfully peel you away from Tarragona’s beach, it will be the neighboring Tarragona Amphitheatre (Amfiteatre de Tarragona). This ancient seaside stadium, constructed in the 2nd century AD, once fit up to 15,000 spectators to cheer on everything from gladiatorial battles to public executions.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Tarragona Amphitheatre in 2024?
A:

Tickets to visit the Tarragona Amphitheatre cost €5 for adults and €2.50 for seniors, students, and children aged 12–17. Children under 12 enter for free. If you're planning more sightseeing in Tarragona, consider a joint ticket that provides entry to five attractions. These start at €15 for adults and €7.50 for concessions.

Q:Are guided tours available at the Tarragona Amphitheatre?
A:

No, Tarragona Amphitheatre does not offer guided tours. However, some city tours include visiting the amphitheater with a guide. You can also download an audio guide on your phone to learn how this large venue was used for the entertainment of ancient Romans. Just be sure to bring headphones.

Q:How long does it take to explore the Tarragona Amphitheatre?
A:

It depends. If you just want to look down at Tarragona Amphitheatre from the publicly accessible areas around the site, you won’t need more than five minutes to do so. However, if you enter the amphitheater, allow at least 30 minutes to walk around and learn about its history.

Q:Is the Tarragona Amphitheatre accessible?
A:

Yes, Tarragona Amphitheatre is partially accessible. The entrance to Tarragona Amphitheatre is accessible by a sloping ramp and the viewing terraces in the ticketed area have even ground with plenty of space. However, access to the amphitheater seating, arena, and exhibits requires going up and down narrow staircases.

Q:Are there any events or reenactments held at the Tarragona Amphitheatre?
A:

Yes, Tarragona Amphitheatre occasionally hosts events, mainly during the festival of Tarraco Viva in May. Tarraco Viva celebrates the city’s ancient Roman history and uses the amphitheater to hold re-enactments of gladiator fights. Other venues around the city host various performances and presentations during this time.
