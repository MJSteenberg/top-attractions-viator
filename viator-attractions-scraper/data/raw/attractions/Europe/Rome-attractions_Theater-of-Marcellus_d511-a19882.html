Theater of Marcellus (Teatro di Marcello) Tours and Tickets

Though this ancient open-air theater resembles a mini-Colosseum, it was built nearly 100 years earlier during the final years of the Roman Republic. Named by the Emperor Augustus in 11 BC after his deceased nephew Marcus Claudius Marcellus, the theater may be the oldest surviving of its kind in the world.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Theater of Marcellus in 2025?
A:

Visiting the Theater of Marcellus is free; it’s part of an outdoor archaeological site you’ll pass while walking through Rome’s Jewish quarter. Seeing it with a guide during a multistop sightseeing tour starts at €49, depending on the tour type and duration.

Q:Can you go inside the Theater of Marcellus?
A:

No, you can’t go inside the Theater of Marcellus. The portion of the structure owned by the city is not accessible to the public, while the top floor consists of private (and very impressive) apartments. However, admiring the theater’s architecture and surrounding ruins of ancient temples is worthwhile.

Q:Are there any concerts or events held at the Theater of Marcellus?
A:

Not exactly. The theater itself is no longer a performance venue; however, some outdoor concerts use the historic site as a backdrop. The Concerti del Tempietto in the summer, for example, combines guided tours of the Theater of Marcellus with music performances just steps away from the ancient attraction.

Q:What is the history of the Theater of Marcellus?
A:

Emperor Augustus dedicated this 20,000-seat theater to his nephew, Marcus Claudius Marcellus, in 13 BC. It was used as a performance venue until the Middle Ages before becoming a fortress, a palace for noble families, and—following a 20th-century restoration—now one of Rome’s best-preserved ancient theaters.

Q:What’s the best time to visit the Theater of Marcellus?
A:

The best times to visit the open-air Theater of Marcellus are early morning (to capture stellar sunrise photos with minimal crowds) or after dark, when the illuminated attraction makes for a lovely evening stroll. The site is particularly nice to visit in spring when daytime temperatures are mild.
