Q:How much does it cost to visit the Aare Gorge in 2024?
A:

Aare Gorge entrance tickets start at 12 Swiss Francs (CHF) for adults and 7.5 Swiss Francs for children ages 615. Many visitors choose to combine a trip to the Aare Gorge with a visit to Reichenbach Falls; a combined entry ticket is 20 Swiss Francs for adults and 14 Swiss Francs for children.

Q:How long does it take to walk through the Aare Gorge?
A:

Many Aare Gorge visitors choose to walk along the trail from the western entrance to the middle of the gorge and then return via the Lammi Pass. This easy stretch is less than a mile (1.4 kilometers) and takes around an hour at a steady pace.

Q:Is the Aare Gorge accessible for people with limited mobility?
A:

Yes, the hiking trail from the western entrance to the middle of the gorge is accessible for wheelchair users and strollers. This part is considered the most beautiful stretch. Avoid returning via the public footpath Lammi Pass as there are some steeper sections and stairs.

Q:Can I combine my visit to the Aare Gorge with other nearby attractions?
A:

Yes, many travelers combine a trip to the Aare Gorge with the nearby Reichenbach Falls, just a few minutes away by car. Other nearby attractions include the Sherlock Museum in Meiringen or Hasliberg Mountain, home to hiking trails and fun-themed walks.

Q:What should I wear or bring when visiting the Aare Gorge?
A:

When visiting the Aare Gorge, wear a light raincoat to protect yourself from the spray, along with a comfy pair of sneakers. On colder days, wrap up warm, as the high limestone walls of the gorge create a shield from the sun, making the temperatures feel significantly cooler.
