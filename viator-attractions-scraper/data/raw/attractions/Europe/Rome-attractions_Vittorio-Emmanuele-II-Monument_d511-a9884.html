People Also Ask
Q:What is Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument (Vittoriano) known for?
A:

The Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument (Vittoriano) is a multi-tiered white marble monument built to honor the first king of unified Italy, Victor Emmanuel II. Overlooking Rome’s busy Piazza di Venezia, it houses the Museo Centrale del Risorgimento, the Sacrario delle Bandiere, exhibition halls, and a scenic glass elevator.

Q:Do you need a ticket to visit Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument?
A:

You don’t need tickets to visit the Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument, although some of the attractions within it require tickets. It’s free for visitors to climb to the first terrace, a popular selfie spot, but you’ll need to pay to access the panoramic terrace—the views are worth it—or if visiting the Museum of the Risorgimento, which explains the history of Italy’s unification.

Q:What is the best time of day to visit Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument?
A:

Much like the Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument itself, the question of when to visit can be divisive. Many people claim that the monument is most attractive at night, when bright illuminations add to its ostentatiousness, while others argue for daytime, when the sun gives the marble a special glow. Here’s a solution: Visit twice.

Q:Can you visit Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument on a guided tour?
A:

Yes, you can visit the Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument on guided tours. It is included as a stop on many hop-on hop-off bus tours, allowing you to spend as long as you’d like here. You’re also likely to stop by the monument during Rome walking tours, whether a private or shared tour.

Q:How long does it take to tour Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument?
A:

That depends. If you’re just popping up to the top to take in the view, or snapping a few quick photos from the first terrace, then half an hour ought to be enough time at the Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument. If you’re visiting the Museum of the Risorgimento or nearby Palazzo Venezia, then count on several hours.

Q:Is Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument accessible?
A:

Yes, Vittorio Emmanuele II Monument is accessible to visitors using wheelchairs or with limited mobility. Both the first-floor terrace—where there’s a coffee shop—and the top-tier panoramic terrace can be reached by elevator.
