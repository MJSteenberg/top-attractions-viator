Syracuse Greek Theater (Teatro Greco) Tours and Tickets

Syracuse’s Greek Theater (Teatro Greco) was one of the largest in the world when it was constructed, able to seat up the 16,000 spectators. Hewn directly from the side of the Temenite Hill overlooking the Sicilian countryside, the stone theater is a highlight of the UNESCO-listed Neapolis Archaeological Park.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Syracuse Greek Theater in 2024?
A:

Standard adult tickets to the Neapolis Archaeological Park—which include entry to the Greek Theater, Roman Amphitheater, and other sights within the park—cost €16.50. You can pay for your admission online or at the on-site ticket office. Guided tours of the park typically cost US$50–US$100 and sometimes visit other attractions.

Q:What is the Syracuse Greek Theater, and why is it significant?
A:

First built on Temenite Hill in the 5th century BC, the Greek Theater is one of Syracuse’s top attractions. The semi-circular stone venue once sat up to 16,000 spectators to see plays by the Greek tragedian Aeschylus. You can relive those ancient times today as the theater puts on classical plays every summer.

Q:Are guided tours available at the Syracuse Greek Theater?
A:

Yes, guided tours are available at the Syracuse Greek Theater (Teatro Greco). You can join archaeologist-led walking tours to learn more about the Greek Theater and other Neapolis Archaeological Park attractions. Guided day trips are another option—several experiences combine the theater with Noto, a baroque town, and Ortigia, the picturesque island and historic center of Syracuse.

Q:How can I buy tickets for performances at the Syracuse Greek (Teatro Greco) Theater?
A:

INDA (National Institute of Ancient Drama) puts on a series of classical plays at the Syracuse Greek Theater (Teatro Greco) in the summer months. You can book your seats on the INDA website—tickets typically go on sale a couple of months before the season begins and are priced based on their proximity to the stage.

Q:Are there nearby attractions to visit along with the Syracuse Greek Theater?
A:

Yes, the Greek Theater (Teatro Greco) is part of Neapolis Archaeological Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient Greek ruins. While you’re there, check out the centuries-old Roman Amphitheater, the sacrificial Altar of Hieron II, old burial chambers like the tomb of the Greek thinker Archimedes, and the Ear of Dionysius, where prisoners were once held.
