Hall of Mirrors (Galerie des Glaces) Tours and Tickets

19,144 reviews

The opulence of the Palace of Versailles reaches its peak in the Hall of Mirrors (Galerie des Glaces)—a 240-foot-long (73-meter-long) ballroom with 357 mirrors adorning 17 huge arches on one side and 17 arcaded windows overlooking the formal gardens on the other. It was also the location of the signing of the Treaty of Versailles, which formally ended World War I.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Hall of Mirrors in 2024?
A:

The Hall of Mirrors is included in a Palace of Versailles ticket, which costs €21 for adults and is free for anyone under 18 years of age and EU residents under 26. If you plan to spend time exploring the entire estate, it’s worth considering a Passport ticket for €32, which covers access to the gardens and the estate of Trianon.

Q:How do I get to the Hall of Mirrors?
A:

The Hall of Mirrors is located inside the Palace of Versailles, about an hour from Paris by car or public transportation. It’s in the main palace building, across the Royal Courtyard, and is well signposted and easy to find, whether on a Grand Apartments self-guided tour or on a guided tour.

Q:Can I buy tickets for the Hall of Mirrors in advance?
A:

Yes, you can buy tickets for the Palace of Versailles, which includes the Hall of Mirrors, in advance—it’s recommended to do so if you’re visiting during the peak summer period. Timed entry tickets are available on the Versailles website. Tours from Paris to Versailles typically include admission to the palatial complex, too.

Q:What is the best month to visit the Hall of Mirrors?
A:

The best months to visit the Hall of Mirrors, and the Palace of Versailles in general, are September–October or March–May. There are often fewer visitors during this time than in the peak summer season, while the weather is typically pleasant enough to enjoy a stroll in the gardens.

Q:Is visiting the Hall of Mirrors worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting the Hall of Mirrors is worth it—this gilded ballroom is considered one of the most beautiful features at the Palace of Versailles, with 357 mirrors decorating 17 massive arches. It's also where the Treaty of Versailles was ratified, which effectively ended World War I.
