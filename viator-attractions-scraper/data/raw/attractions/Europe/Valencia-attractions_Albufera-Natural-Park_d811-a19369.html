Albufera Natural Park (Parque Natural de la Albufera) Tours and Tickets

235 reviews

Albufera, south of Valencia, is home to Spain’s largest lake and some of the country’s most scenic wetlands and lagoons. The park’s natural biodiversity features hundreds of native plants and 340 bird species, including rare and endangered ones. Locally grown rice is featured in many regional dishes, such as paella.

Q:What are the main attractions in Albufera Natural Park?
A:

While there’s much to see in Albufera Natural Park, the park’s main attractions are mostly around L’Albufera Lagoon: It’s here where you can spot the park’s resident birds, go on boat rides, and see traditional cottages known as barraca. The many beaches along the park’s coast are popular, too.

Q:What activities can you do in Albufera Natural Park?
A:

Going on nature walks is the most common activity to do in Albufera Natural Park. Six signposted routes lead you through the park’s different ecosystems, from marshes to dunes and forests. Another popular activity is to take a ride in a handcrafted boat out on L’Albufera Lagoon to see local birds and enjoy the scenery.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit Albufera Natural Park?
A:

Spring is a lovely time of year to go to Albufera Natural Park, when it’s ideal for spotting the park’s resident wildlife, plus it’s not too hot or crowded. Bird-watching enthusiasts might want to wait until the autumn and winter months though, as this is when migratory birds descend on the wetlands.

Q:Are there guided tours available in Albufera Natural Park?
A:

Yes, visitors can take guided tours of Albufera Natural Park from Valencia. Tours typically include a boat ride on the lagoon, bird-watching, visiting traditional houses around the lake, and exploring the different landscapes in the area, including rice paddies and sand dunes.

Q:Can you take boat rides in Albufera Natural Park?
A:

Yes, you can take a boat ride of L’Albufera Lagoon when visiting Albufera Natural Park. Traditional handcrafted boats called albuferenc will explore the lagoon for a better view of its marshes and wetlands and to spot resident birds, often at sunset to see everything at its most picturesque.

Q:Is Albufera Natural Park family-friendly?
A:

Yes, Albufera Natural Park is a family-friendly destination with many different activities for children and parents alike. The park provides a variety of walking trails, ranging from short trails suitable for temperamental little ones to longer, more challenging paths. Beaches line the coast of Albufera Natural Park, which are a popular choice when it’s warm.
