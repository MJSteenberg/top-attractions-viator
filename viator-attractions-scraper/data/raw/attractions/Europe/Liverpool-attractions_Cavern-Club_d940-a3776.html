Q:What is the Cavern Club and why is it famous?
A:

The Cavern Club is a legendary music venue in Liverpool, which opened in 1957 and gained fame in the 1960s when it regularly hosted shows by The Beatles, as well as a host of other famous names. Renowned for its intimate atmosphere and live performances, it remains an iconic symbol of Liverpool's musical heritage and the Merseybeat music scene.

Q:Is the Cavern Club accessible?
A:

Yes, the Cavern Club is an accessible venue. There is a lift into the building and a ramp from the upper to the lower level. The Cavern Club and Cavern Live Lounge areas are accessible, but unfortunately the Cavern Pub is not.

Q:Is it possible to attend live music performances at the Cavern Club?
A:

Yes, it’s possible to attend live music performances at the Cavern Club. The venue continues to host live music events every day from morning until late. The acts vary from established bands to emerging artists and tribute acts, so it's best to check the schedule before visiting.

Q:What famous bands or musicians have performed at the Cavern Club?
A:

Famous bands and musicians that have performed at the Cavern Club include The Beatles, Gerry & the Pacemakers, The Rolling Stones, Elton John, and Stevie Wonder. More recent performers include Oasis, Adele, and Jessie J.

Q:Can visitors purchase food and drinks at the Cavern Club?
A:

Yes, food is available at the Cavern Club, as well as at the Cavern Pub during the daytime. Directly opposite the club is the Cavern Club Restaurant, where the menu includes burgers, chicken wings, fish and chips, light bites, and sharing platters.

Q:Is there parking at the Cavern Club?
A:

No, unfortunately, there's no parking directly at the Cavern Club. However, you can park at Q-Park Liverpool ONE, just a 5-minute walk away. Alternatively, there is pay-and-display parking at North John Street and Victoria Street, providing ample options within walking distance of the venue.
