Mozart’s Birthplace (Mozarts Geburtshaus) Tours and Tickets

679 reviews

The house where Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was born in 1756, Mozart’s Birthplace (Mozart’s Geburtshaus) stands on Getreidegasse, the main shopping street in Salzburg’s Old Town (Altstadt). One of Austria’s most beloved museums, this ocher-painted town house is a must for first-time visitors to the city.

  • Getreidegasse 9, Salzburg, Salzburg, 5020

Q:How much does it cost to visit Mozart’s Birthplace in 2024?
A:

To visit Mozart’s Birthplace, it currently costs €15 for adult tickets, €5 for students, €4.50 for children, or €30 for a family ticket (two adults with children). These rates don’t include a guided tour. There are also combination tickets available that grant entry to both Mozart’s Birthplace and the nearby Mozart Residence.

Q:Are guided tours available at Mozart’s Birthplace?
A:

Yes, many travelers choose to visit Mozart’s Birthplace on a guided tour. These tours are the ideal way to get background information on the life of Mozart and his family. There are a variety of tours available and it’s essential that you book your place ahead of your visit via the website.

Q:Is Mozart’s Birthplace wheelchair-accessible?
A:

No, Mozart’s Birthplace is not accessible to wheelchairs; it's an old building with three stories of exhibition space and in order to preserve the historical nature of the building, it has not been adapted. However, the Mozart Residence is accessible to wheelchair users.

Q:Can I take photos inside Mozart’s Birthplace?
A:

Yes, photography is allowed at Mozart’s Birthplace as long as flash is not used. Filming, however, is not allowed. It is recommended to leave tripods and large items at home as backpacks can’t be checked in, as there are no guarded cloakrooms. Also strollers must be left at the entrance.

Q:What is the best time to visit Mozart’s Birthplace to avoid crowds?
A:

The best time to visit Mozart’s Birthplace to avoid crowds is during the week, ideally close to the opening time or at the end of the day. Afternoons tend to attract the biggest crowds, especially if it’s a weekend during the summer, so it’s advisable to visit outside of these times for a more peaceful experience.
