General Archive of the Indies (Archivo General de Indias) Tours and Tickets

Once considered an important commercial city and port in Europe, Seville built this Renaissance-style building—now called the General Archive of the Indies (Archivo General de Indias)—to serve as a merchant exchange. The space now houses documents related to Spain’s overseas possessions between the 15th and 19th centuries—which comprises more than 6 miles (10 kilometers) of shelving with over 43,000 volumes and 80 million pages.
