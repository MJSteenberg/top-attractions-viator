Maddalena Archipelago (Arcipelago della Maddalena) Tours and Tickets

For deserted lagoons, turquoise waves, and fabulous beaches, set sail for the Maddalena Archipelago (Arcipelago della Maddalena), just off the Costa Smeralda. The group of seven islands and dozens of islets between Sardinia and Corsica is a national park, with crystalline waters for diving and unspoiled coastlines.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Maddalena Archipelago in 2024?
A:

While it’s free to explore the Maddalena Archipelago independently, many travelers prefer to visit with a guided tour that includes boat transfers. There are various tours available, including shared boat tours from Palau starting at €45, multi-island tours for around €110, and private luxury tours at €1,000 per boat.

Q:What are the best ways to get to the Maddalena Archipelago from mainland Sardinia?
A:

From mainland Sardinia, you can get to the Maddalena Archipelago via a ferry from the port of Palau to La Maddalena island; these run throughout the day and take around 20 minutes. For convenience, some travelers join a boat tour or book a private boat rental.

Q:What are the must-see islands in the Maddalena Archipelago?
A:

Some of the must-see islands in the Maddalena Archipelago include La Maddalena, the biggest island and the primary ferry destination; Budelli, which is famed for its pink beach; Caprera, known for its magnificent landscape and picturesque beaches; and Spargi, where you’ll find great snorkeling opportunities.

Q:What is the best time of year to visit the Maddalena Archipelago?
A:

The best time to visit the Maddalena Archipelago is late spring through early fall. Great months include May, September, and October, when it’s typically quieter. The summer tourist crowds usually peak in July and August, when the islands can become very busy.

Q:Do tours of the Maddalena Archipelago include stops for swimming or snorkeling?
A:

Yes, many tours of the Maddalena Archipelago typically include stops for swimming and snorkeling. Tours often visit multiple islands, offering travelers the chance to disembark and spend time on the beaches or in the water. Popular places to go snorkeling include the areas around Caprera, Spargi, and Budelli.
