Q:How much does it cost to visit the Peace Palace in 2025?
A:

While admission to the Peace Palace Visitor Centre is free for visitors, guided tours of the palace require tickets. Tours of the palace interior start at €16.50, while tours of the palace grounds and the Garden Tour are each €12.50. Children under the age of 8 enter for free.

Q:Can I book a guided tour of the Peace Palace?
A:

Yes, visitors can choose from three guided tours of the Peace Palace: the interior, the grounds, or the gardens. The schedules for tours are only made available two weeks in advance, with their frequency and starting times changing depending on when the court is in session. Note that the garden tour is offered in Dutch only, while the other two tours are available in both Dutch and English.

Q:What can I see and do at the Peace Palace?
A:

Visitors to the Peace Palace can learn about the history of the building and the International Court of Justice by visiting the Visitor Centre. Those who choose to take a tour can also see more of the palace—inside or outside—with the interior tour showing off important rooms such as the Great Hall of Justice, the Small Hall of Justice, and the Japanese Room.

Q:Is the Peace Palace accessible?
A:

Not really. The Peace Palace has limited accessibility inside the palace, but also within its grounds, which are connected by many staircases. Mobility scooters are not permitted on guided tours of the palace, while access to people with wheelchairs or walkers is limited. The Visitor Centre has no barriers to entry.

Q:Are there any special exhibitions or events at the Peace Palace?
A:

As the home to the International Court of Justice, the Peace Palace often has a busy calendar of events, although these are not open to the public. An event that the public can enjoy though is the Carillon Concert on Tuesdays and Thursdays at 1pm, when 47 bells in one of the palace towers play for all to hear in Carnegieplein outside the palace gates.
