The historic center also boats the city’s oldest quarter—dating back to 1252—and plays host to some remarkably preserved medieval monuments.

Q:What are the must-see attractions in Stockholm Old Town (Gamla Stan)?
A:

The must-see attractions in Stockholm Old Town (Gamla Stan) include the Royal Palace and Storkyrkan, one of the oldest churches in the Swedish capital. For rainy days, the Nobel Prize Museum and Medeltidsmuseet are great picks, while sunny moments are best enjoyed by people-watching at Stortorget Square, known for its atmospheric charm and colorful townhouses.

Q:How old is Stockholm Old Town?
A:

Stockholm Old Town dates back to the 13th century, however most of the buildings were constructed between the 17th and 18th centuries. Today, along cobbled streets and winding alleyways, you’ll find a bustling hub of cafés, restaurants, museums, and shops.

Q:What are the best ways to explore Stockholm Old Town on foot?
A:

The best way to explore Stockholm Old Town on foot is to begin at the main square of Stortorget. Discover colorful buildings, stop at cafés, visit museums, such as the Nobel Prize Museum, indulge in retail therapy at Vasterlanggatan shopping street, snap photos at the Royal Palace, and visit Storkyrkan church.

Q:What are some recommended restaurants and cafés in Stockholm Old Town?
A:

There are many restaurants and cafés in Stockholm Old Town, and where you choose really depends on your taste preferences. For classic Swedish cuisine, try Tradition or Stockholms Gästabud. Barrels Burgers & Beer serves hearty burgers to fuel you up for your sightseeing, while Restaurant Leijontornet offers high-end dining. For a unique experience, explore the medieval atmosphere of Aifur Krog & Bar with its unusual Viking decor.

Q:Is Stockholm Old Town accessible for wheelchair users and people with mobility issues?
A:

Yes and no, while some parts of Stockholm Old Town are accessible for wheelchair users and people with mobility issues, it does have lots of cobblestones and is hilly in parts. There are flatter streets closer to the edges, towards the waterfront, while the central areas do have areas of uneven surfaces. The nearest metro station, Gamla stan, does have lifts.

Q:Are there any notable events or festivals held in Stockholm Old Town throughout the year?
A:

Yes, Stockholm Old Town hosts several notable events and festivals throughout the year, including live music and cultural events. One of the annual highlights is the Christmas market, which takes place on Stortorget Square and has over 40 stalls selling crafts, food, mulled wine, and handmade festive souvenirs.
