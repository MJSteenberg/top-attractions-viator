Holy Stairs (Scala Sancta) Tours and Tickets

235 reviews

Among the most sacred Catholic sites in Rome, the Scala Santa (or Holy Stairs) is a solemn destination for believers—from pilgrims to popes—who would climb the 28 marble steps on their knees in devotion. The original stairs, which lead to the Chapel of San Lorenzo, are now closed, although a faithful and equally beautiful replica remains open to the public.

People Also Ask
Q:What are the Holy Stairs?
A:

The Holy Stairs in Rome are one of the Catholic faith’s most important pilgrimage sites, comprising 28 marble steps leading to the Chapel of San Lorenzo, also called the Sancta Sanctorum (Holy of Holies). It’s said to be the staircase that Jesus walked up to reach Pontius Pilate’s palace in Jerusalem.

Q:Are the Holy Stairs open to the public?
A:

Yes, the Holy Stairs are open to the public and free to visit daily, though with reduced opening hours on Sundays. The Sancta Sanctorum does require an admission ticket.

Q:Can I take photographs or videos while climbing the Holy Stairs?
A:

No, official rules say that you can’t take photographs or videos while climbing the Holy Stairs. While it may be possible to take photos or videos inside other parts of the sanctuary, it’s better to avoid disturbing one of the most sacred sites in the Catholic religion.

Q:Are there any specific rules or dress codes for visiting the Holy Stairs?
A:

Yes, there are specific rules and dress codes for visiting the Holy Stairs; as is the case in all Catholic churches in Italy, visitors should cover their knees and shoulders before entering. Custom also dictates that visitors must ascend the stairs on their knees.

Q:Are the Holy Stairs worth visiting?
A:

Yes, the Holy Stairs in Rome is worth visiting—it’s considered among the most sacred Catholic pilgrimage sites in the world. Catholic pilgrims, or anyone intrigued by the history of Catholicism, will find the staircase of interest.

Q:Do you need reservations to visit the Holy Stairs?
A:

No, you don’t need reservations to visit the Holy Stairs. Visitors who wish to see and climb the holy staircase can enter the Pontifical Sanctuary of Holy Stairs for free. If you wish to see the Sancta Sanctorum, formerly the pope’s private chapel, you’ll need a ticket which can be booked online.
