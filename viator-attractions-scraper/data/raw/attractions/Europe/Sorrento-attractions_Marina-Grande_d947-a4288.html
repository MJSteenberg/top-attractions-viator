Marina Grande Tours and Tickets

Sorrento's tiny fishing village of Marina Grande is known for its colorful wooden boats bobbing in the harbor, and local fishermen still mend their nets by hand just steps from sunbathers along the beach. At the heart of Marina Grande is the Church of Sant’Anna, dedicated to the town’s patron saint, and a shore lined with small, family-run restaurants serving fresh Mediterranean fish and seafood.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Marina Grande in 2025?
A:

Visiting Marina Grande is free, but activities like boat tours, rentals, and nearby attractions come at a cost. Small-group boat tours range from US$95 to US$300-plus per person. Choose your adventure—whether it’s swimming with a limoncello in hand or discovering hidden coves and secluded beaches along Capri’s coast.

Q:What are the best ways to get to Marina Grande?
A:

You can reach Marina Grande by ferry or private boat from Naples, Sorrento, or other coastal towns. The marina is also accessible by car and is a short drive from the center of Capri.

Q:What can you do at Marina Grande?
A:

At Marina Grande, you can enjoy scenic views, relax by the beach, or take boat tours around the island. There are also plenty of restaurants, cafés, and shops to explore. You can easily book a boat ride to famous spots like the Blue Grotto or Faraglioni Rocks.

Q:Can you rent a boat at Marina Grande?
A:

Yes, boat rentals are available at Marina Grande. Whether you're looking for a small boat to explore the coast on your own or a larger boat with a skipper, there are plenty of options. Prices vary based on the boat type and rental duration.

Q:Is there parking available at Marina Grande?
A:

Yes, parking is available near Marina Grande, but spaces can be limited, especially in peak tourist season. It's best to arrive early or take public transportation to avoid the hassle. If you plan to stay on Capri, you may find accommodations with nearby parking options.
