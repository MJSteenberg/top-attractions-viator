Gornergrat Railway (Gornergrat Bahn) Tours and Tickets

74 reviews

Dating back to 1898, Switzerland’s Gornergrat Railway (Gornergrat Bahn) continues today as Europe’s highest open-air railway. Its train whisks sightseers and skiers from the resort town of Zermatt to the mountain’s 10,135-foot (3,089-meter) station, while providing views of Alpine hamlets, colossal glaciers, and the iconic Matterhorn.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Gornergrat Railway (Gornergrat Bahn) in 2024?
A:

The most popular tourist journey on the Gornergrat Railway (Gornergrat Bahn) is the full Zermatt to Gornergrat route, which teams Matterhorn views with dramatic Alpine scenery. A normal round-trip ticket between the two destinations costs about CHF136 for adults in the summer, and a slightly cheaper CHF102 in winter.

Q:How long does the journey on the Gornergrat Railway take?
A:

The journey between Zermatt and the Gornergrat peak takes just over 33 minutes, passing Findelbach, Riffelalp, Riffelberg, and Rotenboden en route. While most travelers stay for the whole ride up to Gornergrat, the other stations offer interesting stops for access to hiking trails and ski pistes, plus sledding from Rotenboden.

Q:Are there any dining options on the Gornergrat Railway or at the summit?
A:

Yes, most of the stations that line the Gornergrat Railway route have mountain restaurants within easy walking distance. The railway itself also offers dining options onboard, with special offers such as the Nostalchic Class ticket for fine dining in a vintage carriage, or a sunrise journey with an included breakfast.

Q:Is the Gornergrat Railway accessible for travelers with disabilities?
A:

Yes, travelers with reduced mobility disabilities, such as wheelchair users, can travel on the Gornergrat Railway, using the carriage with a low floor that allows for easy boarding and disembarking. At the Gornergrat peak, the Kulmhotel Gornergrat has wheelchair-accessible restrooms. For dining, call the hotel in advance to check for accessible tables.

Q:Can I bring luggage, pets, or sports equipment on the Gornergrat Railway?
A:

Yes, the Gornergrat Railway allows ski equipment and luggage on board, but bikes must be booked in advance for an extra fee. (Each train has space for around 12 bikes.) If you have lots of luggage, there are baggage lockers at Zermatt. Plus, dogs can travel on the Gornergrat Railway for free.
