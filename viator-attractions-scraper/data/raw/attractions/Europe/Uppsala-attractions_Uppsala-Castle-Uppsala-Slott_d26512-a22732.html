Uppsala Castle, a 16th-century fortress in Uppsala, Sweden, played a pivotal role in Swedish history. Built during the reign of King Gustav Vasa, it has hosted royal coronations, assassinations, and abdications. Visitors come here to see the original castle ruins at the Vasaborgen and the on-site Uppsala Art Museum exhibits.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott) in 2024?
A:

Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott) is technically free to visit; however, there are no dedicated visiting facilities for the castle itself. Instead, most visitors come to experience its museums. The Uppsala Art Museum (Uppsala Konstmuseum) is free for all to visit, while the Vasaborgen, dedicated to its ruins, costs around US$12 per adult.

Q:Is there a guided tour available at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott)?
A:

Yes, several guided tours are available at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott). Many tours visit the castle along with Uppsalas other historical highlights and provide rich insights into its centuries-long history. You can also book a guided tour through the Vasaborgen Uppsala Slott, a seasonal museum in the castles original, 16th-century ruins.

Q:Is there a caf or restaurant at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott)?
A:

Yes, there is a restaurant at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott). The old vaults under the Uppsala Art Museum house a restaurant, Cajsas Kitchen (Cajsa's Kk), which serves everything from sandwiches and light lunches to pastries, coffee, and afternoon tea. Its a great place to have a bite in between sightseeing stops.

Q:Are there any discounts for students or children at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott)?
A:

Yes, there are some discounts available at Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott). While the castle and the resident Uppsala Art Museum are free to visit, (apart from some special exhibitions), the Vasaborgen Uppsala Slott offers half-priced (roughly US$6) admission tickets to children, students, and seniors. Note that its only open from June 1September 1.

Q:Is Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott) accessible to disabled visitors?
A:

Owing to its historic architecture, Uppsala Castle (Uppsala Slott) is only partly accessible to disabled visitors. The Uppsala Art Museum is accessible for wheelchair users, including an entry ramp and an elevator between floors. However, the Vasaborgen Museum is not wheelchair-accessible due to its location amidst the castle ruins, and neither is the rooftop.
