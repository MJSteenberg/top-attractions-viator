Q:How much does it cost to visit Marsaxlokk in 2024?
A:

You won’t pay anything to enjoy the scenery and markets of Marsaxlokk, while tours that visit the village are typically affordable. Shared half-day tours that combine a visit with the Blue Grotto start at around €34, while private tours begin at roughly €140 and go up to €295 for a full day of sightseeing.

Q:What are the top attractions to visit in Marsaxlokk?
A:

Marsaxlokk Harbour is the top attraction in the village, thanks to the many colorful traditional fishing boats known as luzzu. Other popular sites include the lively Sunday fish market, the local parish church, and several old fortifications in the area around the village, such as Fort St. Lucian.

Q:Are there guided tours available in Marsaxlokk?
A:

Yes, many guided tours in Malta include Marsaxlokk. The village is often featured in tours that combine it with the Blue Grotto on the south coast, as the two destinations are relatively close. However, these guided tours only allow for a brief stop in Marsaxlokk, typically around one hour.

Q:Is it possible to visit Marsaxlokk as a day trip from other parts of Malta?
A:

Yes, a day trip to Marsaxlokk from places such as Valletta and the Three Cities is quite easy. It normally takes less than 30 minutes to drive from Valletta to Marsaxlokk, while the bus ride is more like one hour; this leaves you with plenty of time to explore the village before you need to head back.

Q:What is the best time to visit Marsaxlokk for the Sunday Fish Market?
A:

If visiting the Marsaxlokk Sunday fish market, it’s best to get there as early as possible. Arrive before dawn and you can watch the fisherman sail into the harbor before the market starts. While the market goes until after noon, the fish and octopus for sale often sell out by mid-morning.
