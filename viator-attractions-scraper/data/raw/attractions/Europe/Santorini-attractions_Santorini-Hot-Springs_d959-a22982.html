Santorini Hot Springs Tours and Tickets

4,955 reviews
Free admission

Santorini’s hot springs are on the tiny, uninhabited islet of Palea Kameni. Continuous volcanic activity underground maintains the springs’ temperature between 86ºF and 95ºF (30°C and 35°C). The sulfuric, orange-tinged spring waters that bubble up into a shallow cove off the islet’s coast are said to be curative for the skin and joints.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Santorini Hot Springs in 2024?
A:

The Santorini Hot Springs are technically a free attraction; the cost applies to the type of tour or transportation you want to use to access them. Many tours exceed US$120, which is typically because they include additional activities. Some luxury tours, both shared and private, start around US$200 and can increase between US$500 and US$2,400.

Q:How can I get to the Santorini Hot Springs?
A:

Most visitors take a boat tour to reach the Santorini Hot Springs. Typically, tours depart from nearby ports Fira, Oia, and Athinios and stop at several additional attractions, like the Nea Kameni volcano or the island of Thirasia. Note that some tours require a short swim to reach the springs.

Q:When is the best time to visit the Santorini Hot Springs?
A:

The best time to visit Santorini Hot Springs is during the warmer months, from late spring to early fall (May to October). During this period the sea temperatures are warmer, making the swimming experience more comfortable. The hot springs aren’t really that hot—the warmer the weather, the hotter the water.

Q:What should I wear to the Santorini Hot Springs?
A:

When visiting the Santorini Hot Springs, wear a swimsuit that you don’t mind getting stained. The waters of the hot springs are rich in minerals (like iron) that can discolor swimwear. You’ll also want water shoes to protect your feet, a towel, a change of clothes, and plenty of sunscreen.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Santorini Hot Springs?
A:

The Santorini Hot Springs are technically free, so the cost to visit depends on the tour or transportation you choose. Some large-group boat tours are roughly the cost of a decent meal, while smaller tours are considerably pricier. Private charters are the most expensive—and most luxurious—option, and their prices vary.

Q:Are the Santorini Hot Springs natural?
A:

Yes, the Santorini Hot Springs are natural—Santorini itself is one giant volcano. The thermal waters are heated by geothermal energy and are believed to have therapeutic properties due to various minerals like sulfur, manganese, and iron. Water temperatures typically max out at 95ºF (35°C), during the warmer months.

Q:Can you go to the Santorini Hot Springs on your own?
A:

Yes, you can go to the Santorini Hot Springs on your own—if you have a boat. The springs are only accessible by water. Tours are the most common way to visit the hot springs, as they include transportation and often provide additional information and context about the area.
