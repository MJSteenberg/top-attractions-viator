Sarajevo Tunnel (Tunel Spasa) Tours and Tickets

The Sarajevo Tunnel was a lifeline to the outside world during the years-long siege of Sarajevo. During the war, the Bosnian army smuggled food and supplies through the tunnel. Today, the small museum serves as a memorial to those difficult times, with information boards, video presentations, and audio guides depicting life under siege.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Sarajevo Tunnel in 2025?
A:

Entrance fees for the Sarajevo Tunnel are 15 KM for adults and 5 KM for students, payable in cash. As the Sarajevo Tunnel is located outside the city center, you may also need to factor in the cost of taking a tour; affordable tours start from around 30.

Q:Can you take a guided tour of the Sarajevo Tunnel?
A:

No, staff at the Sarajevo Tunnel do not provide guided tours, but many tours that focus on Sarajevos war history include a visit here. Alternatively, an audio guide is available to download on smartphones and a 10-minute information video plays on a loop to provide context.

Q:How long does a typical visit to the Sarajevo Tunnel take?
A:

Most visitors find that one hour is plenty of time to visit the Sarajevo Tunnel. You may need to factor more time if you come with a guide or make use of the audio guidetour times typically vary by provider, so check the itinerary in advance.

Q:Is the Sarajevo Tunnel good for kids?
A:

Yes, the Sarajevo Tunnel can be a good place to visit for families with children, so long as theyre not too little. Kids generally find exploring the underground tunnel fun; if they dont, its easy to leave as its relatively short. Older children may find the history interesting to learn about.

Q:Is the Sarajevo Tunnel accessible?
A:

The Sarajevo Tunnel is not accessible for people with wheelchairs or strollers, as there are both stairs and narrow uneven walkways to contend with inside. In the tunnel itself, visitors walk along a narrow cart track with small supports you will need to step over, while also minding the low ceiling.
