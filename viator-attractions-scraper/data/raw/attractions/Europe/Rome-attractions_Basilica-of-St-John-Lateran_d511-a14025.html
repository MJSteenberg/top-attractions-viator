The Basilica of St. John Lateran (Basilica di San Giovanni in Laterano) is the most important of the four major papal basilicas in Rome and is seat of the bishop of Rome: the Pope. One of the most important Catholic churches in the world, the archbasilica is decorated with rich mosaics, Cosmatesque floors, and 12 baroque statues of the apostles.

Q:How much does it cost to visit the Basilica of St. John Lateran in 2024?
A:

If you want to visit the basilica only, then there is no entrance fee; this is a functioning site of worship, so you can simply step inside. That said, there is a 7 charge for visiting the cloister (students and seniors typically receive a slight discount), and a separate fee for audio guides.

Q:Is visiting the Basilica of St. John Lateran worth it?
A:

Yes. While most visitors claim that St. Peters is Romes more impressive basilica, you wont find those hours-long lines at the Basilica of St. John Lateran. That said, the basilica is a 20-minute walk from the Colosseum; so if you have limited time in Rome, you may want to stick to attractions that are closer to the historical city center.

Q:Can you go to the Basilica of St. John Lateran on your own?
A:

Yes, easily. The Basilica of St. John Lateran is located in the San Giovanni neighborhood of Rome, adjacent to the Pontifical Sanctuary of the Holy Stairs, where worshippers ascend a staircase on their knees to revere a Byzantine icon. The area can be reached on foot, by bus, or by metro.

Q:Does the Basilica of St. John Lateran have a dress code?
A:

Yes. Youll have to leave the shorts and tank tops at home, even in the sweltering heat of summer. Modest clothing is required to enter the basilica, so that means keeping shoulders, chests, and knees covered. Unlike elsewhere in Europe, Roman churches have no qualms about turning away inappropriately attired travelers.

Q:Can you take photos in the Basilica of St. John Lateran?
A:

Yes, photography is permitted inside the basilica for personal (meaning non-commercial) use. Refrain from using flash photography, which can damage the paintings and artifacts inside and disturb other visitors. Typically, tripods and selfie sticks are not allowed, and youll have to ask in advance if youre interested in commercial photography.
