Stockholm City Hall (Stadshuset) Tours and Tickets

Stockholm City Hall (Stadshuset) looms over the Kungsholmen waterfront and is one of the capital’s most impressive landmarks, with a redbrick façade and 328-foot (100-meter) tower topped with the Three Crowns of Sweden. Dating back to 1923, the hall contains elaborate ceremonial chambers which are open to visitors via guided tours.

Q:How much does it cost to visit Stockholm City Hall in 2024?
A:

It costs SEK 150 for adults to visit Stockholm City Hall, SEK 120 for senior and student admission, SEK 60 for youths aged 7–18, and children 6 and under can enter for free. This price includes a public guided tour that typically lasts around 45 minutes.

Q:Is it necessary to book Stockholm City Hall tours in advance?
A:

Yes, it’s recommended to book Stockholm City Hall tours in advance. It’s a very popular attraction and can get busy, especially during peak season. Visitor numbers are limited, so it’s best to book in advance to secure your space. Same-day tours may be available but are likely to sell out quickly.

Q:What is included in a tour of Stockholm City Hall?
A:

A tour of Stockholm City Hall typically includes visits to the grand banquet halls—such as the Golden Hall, the Blue Hall, and the Council Chamber. If there aren’t wedding ceremonies, visitors sometimes also visit the Gallery of the Prince. During tours, visitors can see beautiful artwork, tapestries, and architectural highlights.

Q:Can I visit the Stockholm City Hall Tower as part of a guided tour?
A:

Yes, you can visit Stockholm City Hall Tower during a guided tour; however, this only applies to select months of the year. Stockholm City Hall Tower is typically only open during the summer months of May through August. During a visit, it’s possible to climb up the tower for spectacular city views.

Q:Is Stockholm City Hall accessible?
A:

No, as Stockholm City Hall is a historical landmark dating back to 1923, there are limitations that prevent it from being fully accessible. However, there are ramps with handrails in the courtyard and accessible bathrooms. Certain parts of the building, such as the second half of the City Hall Tower, aren’t fully accessible.
