Q:How much does it cost to visit Zlatni Rat Beach (Golden Horn) in 2024?
A:

While there is no entrance fee to visit Zlatni Rat Beach itself, there are costs to get there. Boat tours out to the beach can range from around US$80 per person on a group speedboat tour or closer to US$400 per group for a private excursion.

Q:What are the best times to visit Zlatni Rat Beach (Golden Horn)?
A:

The best time of day to visit Zlatni Rat Beach is first thing in the morning, especially if you want to secure a spot on the beach. The weather in July and August is great for swimming, but expect crowds. Early September is an excellent alternative, which is typically much quieter.

Q:How do I get to Zlatni Rat Beach (Golden Horn)?
A:

Zlatni Rat Beach is located on Brac Island, a boat or ferry ride away from Split or Trogir. There are plenty of tour options that will get you to the beach, from private DIY tours that allow you to create your own itinerary, to small-group tours that visit Brac, Pakleni, Hvar, and Solta.

Q:Is visiting Zlatni Rat Beach worth it?
A:

Yes, visiting Zlatni Rat Beach is worth it. The crystalline waters are typically calm, at least by the shore, and itâ€™s a lovely place to swim, relax, and snorkel. Westerly winds in the afternoon provide ideal conditions for windsurfing, too.

Q:Are there facilities at Zlatni Rat Beach?
A:

Yes, there are plenty of facilities at Zlatni Rat Beach, including kiosks selling food and drinks, restaurants, and vendors renting beach chairs and parasols. There are on-site restrooms, but you need to pay to use them, so bring coins. Lifeguards are stationed at the beach during the summer months.
