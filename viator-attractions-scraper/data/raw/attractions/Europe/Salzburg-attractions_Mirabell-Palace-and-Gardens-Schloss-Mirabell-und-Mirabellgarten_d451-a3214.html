Mirabell Palace and Gardens (Schloss Mirabell und Mirabellgarten) Tours and Tickets

Part of Salzburg’s UNESCO World Heritage–listed historic center, Mirabell Palace (Schloss Mirabell) enjoys a rich royal history, as well as a place in movie legend: it was one of the filming sites forThe Sound of Music. Built by Prince Archbishop Wolf Dietrich in 1606, the palace is most famous for its magnificent baroque gardens.

Q:Is Mirabell Palace worth visiting?
A:

Yes, Mirabell Palace and Gardens is worth visiting—it’s one of Salzburg’s most popular attractions. UNESCO-designated, the palace is an exquisite example of Baroque architecture, and surrounding it is one of the most beautiful Baroque gardens in Europe. What’s more, few palaces and gardens are this easy to get to and free of charge.

Q:How long do you need at Mirabell Palace?
A:

Your time at Mirabell Palace will vary depending on your interests and what you want to see. Generally, visitors spend 1-2 hours exploring the palace and its gardens. However, if you're particularly interested in the palace details or want to spend time strolling through the gardens, you may want to allocate more time.

Q:What can you see inside Mirabell Palace?
A:

Most rooms inside Mirabell Palace are still in use and are thus closed to the public. Visitors are allowed up the “Donnerstiege” (“staircase of thunder”) and into the Marble Hall, the former banquet hall of the prince-archbishops and one of Europe’s finest wedding venues. The Palace Chapel and Hedge Theatre are also open to the public.

Q:Are Mirabell Gardens free?
A:

Yes, Mirabell Gardens are free to enter and explore. Visitors can enjoy the meticulously manicured gardens, sculptures, and fountains (including the Pegasus Fountain featured in The Sound of Music), plus certain areas of the palace, roughly from dawn to dusk. The classical concerts offered at night do, however, require paid tickets.

Q:What is the history behind Mirabell Palace and Gardens?
A:

Mirabell Palace was built in 1606 by Prince-Archbishop Wolf Dietrich von Raitenau for his mistress, Salome Alt. Since its construction, it has housed multiple kings, princes, archbishops, and more. The gardens were redesigned in the Baroque style in the early 18th century and have since become one of the world's most famous and beloved gardens.

Q:When’s the best time to visit Mirabell Palace and Gardens?
A:

The Mirabell Palace and Gardens are one of Salzburg’s most popular attractions. To avoid the crowds, visit on a weekday, early in the morning or later in the evening—the gardens are typically open from roughly 6am to 6pm. The palace has more limited hours; if you wish to visit both, plan accordingly.
