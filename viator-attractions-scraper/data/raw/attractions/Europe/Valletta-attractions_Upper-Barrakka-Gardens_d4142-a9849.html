Q:How much does it cost to visit the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela in 2025?
A:

Tickets start at €12 for museum entry and to see Pórtico de la Gloria, while it's €20 to visit the Carraca's Tower and roof. The cathedral offers limited free tickets to visit the Pórtico de la Gloria in the afternoon up to seven days in advance.

Q:Are guided tours available at the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela?
A:

Yes, guided tours to the Pórtico de la Gloria in the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela are available. When booked through the cathedral, they are normally in Spanish. Outside tour companies offer English tours. Tours of Carraca's Tower and the Cathedral roof are only available on the official website.

Q:Do I need to book tickets in advance for the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela?
A:

No, but booking timed tickets in advance is recommended, as prebooking lets you visit when you wish. You must book in advance to get the free Pórtico de la Gloria tickets, available up to seven days in advance online or the evening before at the ticket desk.

Q:Is there a dress code for visiting the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela?
A:

The Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela is a place of worship, and visitors are expected to dress respectfully—no bare shoulders or short shorts or skirts. However, the cathedral sees many pilgrims on long journeys, and its staff is often forgiving of those wearing shorts, T-shirts, and hiking boots.

Q:Can I attend a mass at the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela?
A:

Yes, visitors can attend mass at the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela. A regular pilgrim’s mass is scheduled for those walking the Camino de Santiago. Pilgrim’s mass is held four times per day in Spanish, while mass in English is held at 10:30am in the Pilgrim’s Office Chapel.
